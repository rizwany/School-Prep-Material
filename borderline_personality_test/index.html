Learn More About Borderline Personality–Related Traits

Traits associated with borderline personality patterns — intense emotions, fear of abandonment, unstable relationships, and impulsivity — exist on a spectrum, and most people recognize at least some of these tendencies during stressful periods. Understanding these patterns is about building self-awareness and emotional regulation, not labeling yourself.

Evidence-based approaches like Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) show that emotional regulation and interpersonal effectiveness are skills that can be learned and strengthened over time, regardless of where you currently score.

Ways to understand and support yourself:

  • Learn DBT Skills Basics: Explore the four DBT skill modules (mindfulness, distress tolerance, emotion regulation, interpersonal effectiveness) through reputable psychoeducation resources.
  • Practice Naming Emotions: When you feel a strong reaction, pause and name the specific emotion (not just "bad" or "good") — this alone reduces emotional intensity.
  • Use a Feelings Diary: Track mood swings, triggers, and urges daily; diary cards are a well-studied way to spot patterns over time.
  • Seek Professional Support: If these patterns cause real distress or relationship difficulty, a therapist trained in DBT or schema therapy can offer structured, personalized support.
