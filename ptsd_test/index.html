Learn More About PTSD Symptom Reflection

Post-traumatic stress reactions can follow exposure to a distressing or life-threatening event, and may include intrusive memories, avoidance, negative changes in mood or thinking, and heightened alertness. A brief reflection like this can raise awareness of patterns, but only a qualified professional can properly assess trauma-related conditions.

Trauma responses are the nervous system's attempt to protect you, even when they no longer feel helpful — and evidence-based treatments (like trauma-focused CBT and EMDR) have strong success rates in reducing these symptoms over time.

Ways to understand and support yourself:

  • Learn the Symptom Clusters: Understanding the four main categories (intrusion, avoidance, negative mood/cognition changes, and hyperarousal) helps you recognize your own patterns more clearly.
  • Practice Grounding Techniques: Simple sensory grounding (naming things you can see, hear, touch) can help interrupt distressing flashbacks or intrusive memories in the moment.
  • Build a Safety and Support Plan: Identify trusted people and resources you can reach out to before you're in crisis, not during one.
  • Seek a Trauma-Informed Professional: Ask about evidence-based approaches like trauma-focused CBT, EMDR, or somatic therapies.
