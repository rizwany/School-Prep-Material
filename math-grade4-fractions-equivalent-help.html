Fractions — Equivalent & Comparing

Two fractions can look completely different yet stand for the same amount — they are equivalent fractions. This guide shows you how to find them, simplify fractions to their simplest form, and compare fractions confidently.

⏱ ~6 min read 📝 Jump to Worksheet →

Section 1

What are Equivalent Fractions?

Equivalent fractions are different fractions that represent the same value. For example, ½, 2/4, 3/6, and 4/8 are all equivalent — they all mean "half".

A fraction has two parts: the numerator (top number — how many pieces you have) and the denominator (bottom number — how many equal pieces in total).

12 = 24 = 36 = 48

Section 2

How to Find Equivalent Fractions

  1. 1
    Multiply both numerator and denominator by the same number. This is like multiplying by 1, so the value doesn't change. e.g. ¾ × 3/3 = 9/12.
  2. 2
    Divide both numerator and denominator by the same number (a common factor). This simplifies the fraction. e.g. 6/8 ÷ 2/2 = 3/4.
  3. 3
    Simplest form (lowest terms): a fraction is in its simplest form when the only common factor of numerator and denominator is 1.

Section 3

How to Compare Fractions

Same denominator: compare numerators directly. 3/8 > 2/8 because 3 > 2.

Same numerator: the fraction with the smaller denominator is bigger. 1/3 > 1/5 because thirds are bigger slices than fifths.

Different numerator & denominator: convert to a common denominator, then compare. Or use cross-multiplication: for a/b vs c/d, calculate a×d and c×b — the bigger product wins.

Section 4

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Building equivalent fractions
Find three fractions equivalent to 1/2.
1/2 × 2/2 = 2/4
1/2 × 3/3 = 3/6
1/2 × 5/5 = 5/10
✅ 2/4, 3/6, and 5/10 are all equivalent to 1/2
Example 2 — Find the missing numerator
Fill in the blank: 3/4 = ?/12
Denominator went from 4 to 12 → multiplied by 3 (12 ÷ 4 = 3)
So numerator also × 3: 3 × 3 = 9
✅ Answer: 9/12
Example 3 — Simplify to simplest form
Simplify 6/8.
Common factor of 6 and 8: 2 (both divisible by 2)
6 ÷ 2 = 3    8 ÷ 2 = 4
✅ Simplest form: 3/4
Example 4 — Compare two fractions
Which is greater: 2/3 or 3/5?
Cross-multiply: 2×5=10 and 3×3=9
10 > 9, so 2/3 > 3/5
✅ 2/3 is greater than 3/5
Example 5 — Order fractions from smallest to largest
Order these from smallest to largest: 1/4, 1/2, 3/8.
Convert to denominator 8: 1/4=2/8, 1/2=4/8, 3/8=3/8
Compare numerators: 2/8 < 3/8 < 4/8
✅ Smallest to largest: 1/4, 3/8, 1/2

Section 5

Watch Out! ⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Bigger denominator = bigger fraction: Thinking 1/8 > 1/3 because 8 > 3.
    ✅ A bigger denominator means MORE pieces — so each piece is SMALLER. 1/8 < 1/3.
  • ❌ Adding top and bottom separately to find equivalent fractions: Writing 1/2 → 2/3 (adding 1 to both).
    ✅ You must MULTIPLY (or divide) both by the SAME number, not add the same number.
  • ❌ Stopping simplification too early: Simplifying 12/16 to 6/8 and thinking you're done.
    ✅ Keep simplifying until no common factor other than 1 exists. 12/16 → 6/8 → 3/4 ✓

Section 6

Quick Reference

Equivalent fractions
Multiply or divide both numerator and denominator by the same number.
Simplest form
No common factor (other than 1) between numerator and denominator.
Same denominator
Compare numerators: bigger numerator = bigger fraction.
Same numerator
Smaller denominator = bigger fraction (fewer, larger pieces).
Cross-multiply
a/b vs c/d: compare a×d with c×b. Bigger product is the bigger fraction.
Common denominator
Rewrite both fractions with the same denominator, then compare numerators.
