Number Before & After

In this guide, you'll learn how to find the number that comes just before or just after any number, and how to find the number that sits between two numbers.

⏱ ~5 min read 📝 Jump to Worksheet →

Section 1

Before, After, and Between

Numbers live in a line — always in the same order. Every number has a neighbour before it (1 less) and a neighbour after it (1 more).

Before means 1 less (to the left on the number line).
After means 1 more (to the right on the number line).
Between means the number in the middle of two neighbours.

7BEFORE
8
9AFTER

7 is just before 8.   9 is just after 8.   8 is between 7 and 9.

On the number line, "before" is to the left and "after" is to the right.

Section 2

How to Find Before & After

  1. 1
    To find what comes BEFORE: Subtract 1 from the number. Before 15 → 15 − 1 = 14.
  2. 2
    To find what comes AFTER: Add 1 to the number. After 15 → 15 + 1 = 16.
  3. 3
    To find what is BETWEEN two numbers: The number in the middle. Between 14 and 16 → 15.
  4. 4
    Watch the decade change! Before 20 is 19 (not 20−1=21!). When you cross a ten, the tens digit changes.

Section 3

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Number just after
What number comes just after 36?
36 + 1 = 37 add 1
✅ Answer: 37
Example 2 — Number just before
What number comes just before 50?
50 − 1 = 49 subtract 1
✅ Answer: 49
Example 3 — Number between
What number is between 22 and 24?
22, ___, 24
22 + 1 = 23 the middle number
✅ Answer: 23
Example 4 — Crossing a ten
What number comes just after 99?
99 + 1 = 100 the next number after 99 is a new hundred!
✅ Answer: 100
Example 5 — Word problem
Sara is on step 17 of a staircase. She takes one step up. What step is she on now?
One step up = one number after
17 + 1 = 18
✅ Answer: Step 18

Section 4

Watch Out! ⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Forgetting the decade crossover: Saying the number before 30 is 21
    ✅ Before 30 is 29. You go back into the 20s: 28, 29, 30.
  • ❌ Mixing up before and after: Thinking "after" means a smaller number
    ✅ After always means MORE (bigger). Before always means LESS (smaller).
  • ❌ "Between" asking for two numbers: Giving 22 AND 24 when asked what is between them
    ✅ Between 22 and 24 there is exactly ONE number: 23.

Section 5

Quick Reference

Just Before
The number − 1. Before 45 → 44.
Just After
The number + 1. After 45 → 46.
Between
The middle number. Between 44 and 46 → 45.
Neighbours
Every number has two neighbours — one on each side.
Decade crossover
Before 10 → 9. Before 20 → 19. Before 100 → 99.
Number line tip
Left = smaller (before). Right = bigger (after).
