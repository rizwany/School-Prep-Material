Fractions Intro

In this guide, you'll learn what fractions are, how to read and write ½, ⅓, ¼ and more, and how to compare fractions using visual fraction bars.

Section 1

What is a Fraction?

A fraction represents a part of a whole. When you cut a pizza into 4 equal slices and eat 1, you've eaten ¼ (one quarter) of the pizza.

Every fraction has two parts: the numerator (top number) tells you how many parts you have. The denominator (bottom number) tells you how many equal parts the whole is split into.

The denominator must always be more than zero, and both parts must be whole numbers.

Section 2

Step-by-Step Method

Numerator / Denominator
Numerator = parts you have  |  Denominator = total equal parts
  1. 1
    Find the denominator first. Count the total number of equal parts the shape or set is divided into. This is the bottom number.
  2. 2
    Find the numerator. Count how many parts are shaded, selected, or described. This is the top number.
  3. 3
    Write the fraction. Place the numerator above a line, denominator below. E.g. 2 parts out of 5 = 2/5.
  4. 4
    Comparing fractions: When denominators are the same, the bigger numerator = bigger fraction. When numerators are the same, the smaller denominator = bigger fraction.

Section 3

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Reading a Fraction
A rectangle is divided into 4 equal parts. 3 parts are shaded. What fraction is shaded?
Total equal parts = 4 ← denominator
Shaded parts = 3 ← numerator
Fraction = 3/4
✅ Answer: ¾ (three quarters)
Example 2 — Fraction of a Set
There are 6 apples. 2 of them are red. What fraction are red?
Total apples = 6 ← denominator
Red apples = 2 ← numerator
Fraction = 2/6
✅ Answer: 2/6 (two sixths)
Example 3 — Comparing Fractions (Same Denominator)
Which is greater: 3/8 or 5/8?
Same denominator (8), so compare numerators
3 < 5
So 5/8 > 3/8
✅ Answer: 5/8 is greater
Example 4 — Comparing Fractions (Same Numerator)
Which is greater: ½ or ¼?
Same numerator (1), so compare denominators
Smaller denominator = larger piece
2 < 4, so ½ > ¼
✅ Answer: ½ is greater
Example 5 — Word Problem
Mia ate 2 slices of a pizza cut into 6 equal slices. What fraction of the pizza did she eat?
Total slices = 6 ← denominator
Slices eaten = 2 ← numerator
Fraction eaten = 2/6
✅ Answer: 2/6 of the pizza

Section 4

Watch Out! ⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Swapping numerator and denominator: Writing 4/3 instead of 3/4
    ✅ Numerator (top) = parts you have. Denominator (bottom) = total parts.
  • ❌ Thinking bigger denominator = bigger fraction: Saying ⅛ > ½
    ✅ More equal parts means each part is smaller. ½ is always bigger than ⅛.
  • ❌ Parts must be equal: Cutting a shape unevenly and still calling it ½
    ✅ For fractions to work, ALL parts of the whole must be the same size.

Section 5

Quick Reference

Key Fractions to Know
½ (one half)
1 part out of 2 equal parts.
⅓ (one third)
1 part out of 3 equal parts.
¼ (one quarter)
1 part out of 4 equal parts.
¾ (three quarters)
3 parts out of 4 equal parts.
Numerator
Top number — how many parts you have.
Denominator
Bottom number — total equal parts.
