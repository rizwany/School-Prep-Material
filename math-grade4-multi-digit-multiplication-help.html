Multi-digit Multiplication

Multiplying larger numbers is simply short multiplication done in layers. This guide walks you through the standard algorithm — multiply by ones, multiply by tens, then add the two partial products — with five clear worked examples.

⏱ ~6 min read 📝 Jump to Worksheet →

Section 1

What is Multi-digit Multiplication?

Multi-digit multiplication means multiplying numbers that have two or more digits — for example, 23 × 15 or 134 × 6.

Instead of trying to calculate the whole thing at once, we break it into smaller multiplications called partial products, then add them together.

The method below is called the standard algorithm and works for any size numbers.

Section 2

How to Multiply (Standard Algorithm)

  1. 1
    Write the larger number on top, smaller below. Line up digits in columns.
  2. 2
    Multiply the top number by the ones digit of the bottom number. Write the result below the line. Carry any extra tens as usual.
  3. 3
    Multiply the top number by the tens digit of the bottom number. Write this result on the next line — but start it one place to the left (shift left by one). Use a zero placeholder in the ones column.
  4. 4
    Add the two partial products. The sum is your final answer.

Section 3

Worked Examples

Example 1 — 23 × 4 (2-digit × 1-digit)
Work out 23 × 4.
Ones: 4 × 3 = 12 → write 2, carry 1
Tens: 4 × 2 = 8, plus carry 1 = 9
✅ Answer: 92
Example 2 — 135 × 3 (3-digit × 1-digit)
Work out 135 × 3.
Ones: 3 × 5 = 15 → write 5, carry 1
Tens: 3 × 3 = 9, plus carry 1 = 10 → write 0, carry 1
Hundreds: 3 × 1 = 3, plus carry 1 = 4
✅ Answer: 405
Example 3 — 23 × 15 (2-digit × 2-digit)
Work out 23 × 15.
Step 1 — multiply by 5 (ones): 23 × 5 = 115
Step 2 — multiply by 1 (tens): 23 × 10 = 230 (write 0 placeholder in ones)
Add partial products: 115 + 230 = 345
✅ Answer: 345
Example 4 — 46 × 32 (2-digit × 2-digit)
Work out 46 × 32.
Step 1 — multiply by 2 (ones): 46 × 2 = 92
Step 2 — multiply by 3 (tens): 46 × 30 = 1,380 (shift left; 46×3=138, add zero)
Add partial products: 92 + 1,380 = 1,472
✅ Answer: 1,472
Example 5 — Word Problem
A school orders 24 boxes of pencils. Each box contains 12 pencils. How many pencils in total?
24 × 12
Step 1: 24 × 2 = 48
Step 2: 24 × 10 = 240
Total: 48 + 240 = 288
✅ Answer: 288 pencils

Section 4

Watch Out! ⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Forgetting the zero placeholder: When multiplying by the tens digit, not shifting the row left.
    ✅ Always write a 0 in the ones column of the second partial product before writing anything else.
  • ❌ Losing a carry: Writing 4 × 6 = 24, but then forgetting to add the carried 2 to the next column.
    ✅ Write the carry digit small above the next column immediately — don't hold it in your head.
  • ❌ Adding before you finish both rows: Adding partial products before the second row is complete.
    ✅ Complete both rows fully, then add them. Do not rush the addition step.

Section 5

Quick Reference

Partial product
The result of multiplying the top number by just one digit of the bottom number.
Zero placeholder
Write a 0 in the ones column of the second row to show you're multiplying by tens.
Order of rows
Row 1 = top × ones digit. Row 2 = top × tens digit (shifted one left).
Carrying
When a column product ≥ 10, write the ones digit and carry the tens digit to the next column.
Check
Swap the two numbers and multiply again — the answer should be identical (commutative property).
Estimate first
Round both numbers to the nearest 10 and multiply to get a rough check: 23×15 ≈ 20×15 = 300.
