Linear Equations in Two Variables

Grade 9 Math  ·  Solving systems by substitution & elimination

💡 Standard Form

A linear equation in two variables has the form ax + by + c = 0, where a, b, c are real numbers and a, b are not both zero.

A solution is an ordered pair (x, y) that satisfies the equation. A single linear equation has infinitely many solutions — each one is a point on a straight-line graph.

A system of two linear equations usually has one unique solution — the point where the two lines intersect.

📊 Types of Solutions

One Solution
Lines intersect at one point. Consistent & independent.
No Solution
Parallel lines — never meet. Inconsistent system.
Infinite Solutions
Same line. Consistent & dependent.

🔧 Solution Methods

Substitution Method
  1. Isolate one variable in either equation.
  2. Substitute that expression into the other equation.
  3. Solve the resulting single-variable equation.
  4. Back-substitute to find the second variable.
Elimination Method
  1. Multiply equations so one variable has equal (opposite) coefficients.
  2. Add (or subtract) the equations to eliminate that variable.
  3. Solve for the remaining variable.
  4. Substitute back to find the other variable.

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Check if (2, 3) is a solution of 3x − y = 3

1

Substitute x = 2, y = 3: 3(2) − 3 = 6 − 3 = 3. ✓

(2, 3) IS a solution
2

Solve by substitution: x + y = 10 and x − y = 2

1

From equation 1: x = 10 − y.

2

Substitute into equation 2: (10 − y) − y = 2 → 10 − 2y = 2 → y = 4.

3

Back-substitute: x = 10 − 4 = 6.

x = 6, y = 4
3

Solve by elimination: 2x + 3y = 12 and 2x − y = 4

1

Subtract equation 2 from equation 1 to eliminate 2x: (2x + 3y) − (2x − y) = 12 − 4.

2

4y = 8 → y = 2.

3

Substitute into equation 2: 2x − 2 = 4 → x = 3.

x = 3, y = 2
4

Word problem: Sum of two numbers is 20; one is 4 more than the other. Find them.

1

Let x and y be the numbers. Equations: x + y = 20 and x = y + 4.

2

Substitute: (y + 4) + y = 20 → 2y = 16 → y = 8.

3

x = 8 + 4 = 12.

The numbers are 12 and 8
5

Are 3x + 6y = 12 and x + 2y = 5 consistent?

1

Divide equation 1 by 3: x + 2y = 4.

2

Compare with equation 2: x + 2y = 5. Both describe the same left side but different right sides.

3

The lines are parallel — no intersection.

No solution — inconsistent system

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • CHECKAlways verify your solution in BOTH original equations, not just one.
  • SIGNWhen subtracting an equation in elimination, change the sign of EVERY term: −(2x − y) = −2x + y.
  • MULTIPLYIf coefficients don't match, multiply one or both equations before eliminating.
  • LABELIn word problems, always define your variables clearly before writing equations.

📋 Quick Reference

MethodBest used when…
SubstitutionOne variable is already isolated or has coefficient 1
EliminationBoth equations have matching (or easily matched) coefficients
