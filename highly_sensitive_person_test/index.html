Learn More About Highly Sensitive Person Traits

High sensitivity (sensory processing sensitivity) is a well-documented personality trait found in roughly 15-20% of people, involving deeper processing of sensory input, stronger emotional reactivity, and greater awareness of subtleties others might miss. It is not a disorder — many highly sensitive people are deeply empathetic, creative, and perceptive.

Because highly sensitive people process more deeply, they're also more prone to overstimulation and burnout — understanding this trait is mostly about designing a routine that works with your nervous system instead of against it.

Ways to understand and support yourself:

  • Build in Recovery Time: Schedule quiet downtime after socially or sensorily intense events rather than stacking commitments back-to-back.
  • Identify Your Personal Triggers: Notice which specific inputs (noise, crowds, bright light, conflict) overwhelm you fastest, and reduce exposure where you can.
  • Reframe Sensitivity as Data, Not a Flaw: Your reactions often pick up on real information others miss; use that awareness deliberately in relationships and decisions.
  • Learn Simple Grounding Techniques: Practice a quick reset (slow breathing, stepping outside, noise-canceling headphones) for moments of overstimulation.
