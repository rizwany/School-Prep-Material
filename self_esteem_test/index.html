Learn More About Self-Esteem

Self-esteem is your overall sense of your own worth — how you evaluate yourself across competence, relationships, and character. Healthy self-esteem isn't about feeling superior; it's a stable, realistic sense of worth that doesn't collapse with every setback or criticism.

Self-esteem is shaped over years, but research on self-compassion and cognitive reframing shows it can be actively rebuilt at any stage of life through consistent practice.

To build this skill step-by-step:

  • Practice Self-Compassion, Not Self-Criticism: When you make a mistake, ask "what would I say to a friend in this situation?" and try saying that to yourself.
  • Track Small Wins Daily: Keep a simple log of things you did well or are proud of, however small; this counteracts the mind's bias toward remembering failures more.
  • Challenge All-or-Nothing Self-Talk: Notice absolute language ("I always mess up") and consciously replace it with more accurate, specific language.
  • Set and Keep Small Promises to Yourself: Genuine self-esteem grows from evidence, not affirmations; following through on small commitments builds real self-trust.
