Polynomials

Grade 9 Math  ·  Degree, operations, Remainder & Factor Theorems

💡 What is a Polynomial?

A polynomial is an expression of the form aₙxⁿ + aₙ₋₁xⁿ⁻¹ + … + a₁x + a₀, where the exponents are non-negative integers and the coefficients are real numbers.

The degree is the highest power of x. The leading coefficient is the coefficient of the highest-degree term.

Example: 3x⁴ − 2x² + 5 has degree 4 and leading coefficient 3.

By degree — Linear (degree 1), Quadratic (degree 2), Cubic (degree 3).

📜 Remainder & Factor Theorems

Remainder Theorem
When p(x) is divided by (x − a), remainder = p(a)

To find the remainder when dividing p(x) by (x − a), substitute x = a into p(x). No long division needed.

Factor Theorem
(x − a) is a factor of p(x) if and only if p(a) = 0

If p(a) = 0, then (x − a) divides p(x) exactly — it is a factor. Use this to find factors by testing integer values.

Find the remainder when p(x) = x³ − 4x + 6 is divided by (x − 2)

Remainder Theorem: substitute x = 2.

2

p(2) = (2)³ − 4(2) + 6 = 8 − 8 + 6 = 6.

Remainder = 6

Is (x − 1) a factor of p(x) = x³ − 3x² + 3x − 1?

1

Factor Theorem: evaluate p(1).

2

p(1) = 1 − 3 + 3 − 1 = 0.

3

Since p(1) = 0, (x − 1) IS a factor.

Yes, (x − 1) is a factor
5

Multiply (x + 3)(x² − 2x + 4)

1

Distribute x: x · x² = x³, x · (−2x) = −2x², x · 4 = 4x.

2

Distribute 3: 3 · x² = 3x², 3 · (−2x) = −6x, 3 · 4 = 12.

3

Combine: x³ + (−2x² + 3x²) + (4x − 6x) + 12 = x³ + x² − 2x + 12.

x³ + x² − 2x + 12

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • LIKE TERMSOnly add/subtract terms with the same variable and exponent: 3x² and 5x are NOT like terms.
  • DEGREEThe degree is the highest power, not the number of terms: 5x⁴ − 1 has degree 4, not 2.
  • REMAINDERFor divisor (x + a), substitute x = −a (not +a): remainder of p(x) ÷ (x + 3) is p(−3).
  • SUBTRACTIONWhen subtracting polynomials, distribute the minus sign to EVERY term: (x² − 3x) − (2x² + x) = x² − 3x − 2x² − x.
