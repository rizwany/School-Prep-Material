Percentage Calculations

Grade 6 Math  ·  Finding percentages and applying them in real life

💯 What is a Percentage?

Percent means "per hundred." So 35% means 35 out of every 100.

% of a Number

Find P% of n: multiply n by P then divide by 100

Express as %

Find what % one number is of another: (part ÷ whole) × 100

% Increase

(Increase ÷ original) × 100. New value = original + increase

% Decrease

(Decrease ÷ original) × 100. New value = original − decrease

📐 Key Formulas

Find P% of a number n
P% of n = (P ÷ 100) × n
Express part as a percentage of whole
Percentage = (part ÷ whole) × 100
Percentage increase/decrease
% change = (amount of change ÷ original value) × 100
New value after % change
Increase: new = original × (1 + P/100)  |  Decrease: new = original × (1 − P/100)

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Find 30% of 80

1

Use the formula: (30 ÷ 100) × 80

2

0.30 × 80 = 24

30% of 80 = 24
2

15 is what percentage of 60?

1

Percentage = (part ÷ whole) × 100

2

(15 ÷ 60) × 100 = 0.25 × 100 = 25%

15 is 25% of 60
3

A price rises from £40 to £48. What is the percentage increase?

1

Amount of increase = 48 − 40 = 8

2

% increase = (8 ÷ 40) × 100 = 20%

Percentage increase = 20%
4

A jacket costs £120. There is a 15% discount. What is the sale price?

1

Find 15% of £120: (15 ÷ 100) × 120 = £18 discount

2

Sale price = £120 − £18 = £102

Sale price = £102
5

A school has 450 pupils. 40% are in Key Stage 2. How many is that?

1

(40 ÷ 100) × 450 = 0.4 × 450 = 180

180 pupils are in Key Stage 2

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • ORIGINAL VALUE Percentage increase and decrease always use the original value as the denominator — not the new value.
  • CONVERT % To use a percentage in a calculation, divide by 100 first. 35% = 0.35
  • PART vs WHOLE When expressing part as %, make sure part ÷ whole (not whole ÷ part). Order matters!
  • SALE PRICE A 15% discount doesn't give you the discount — it gives you what's removed. Subtract it from the original to get the sale price.
