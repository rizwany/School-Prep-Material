Percentages Intro

Grade 5 Math  ·  Out of 100 — fractions in disguise

💡 What is a Percentage?

The word percent comes from Latin per centum — meaning "per hundred." A percentage tells you how many out of every 100.

Definition
n% = n out of 100 = n/100

For example, 75% means 75 out of 100, or 75/100, or 0.75.

75%

The whole (100%) bar above has 75% filled in blue — that's what 75% looks like.

🔄 Converting Between %, Fractions & Decimals

% → Fraction
n% = n/100 (simplify)
% → Decimal
n% = n ÷ 100 (move point left 2)
Fraction → %
a/b → (a÷b) × 100
Decimal → %
0.nn → multiply by 100

🧮 Finding a Percentage of a Number

To find p% of n, multiply n by p/100 (or equivalently by the decimal form of p%).

Formula
p% of n = (p ÷ 100) × n

Example: 30% of 80 = (30 ÷ 100) × 80 = 0.3 × 80 = 24

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Write 40% as a fraction and a decimal

1

Fraction: 40% = 40/100 = 2/5 (simplify by dividing by 20)

2

Decimal: 40 ÷ 100 = 0.40 = 0.4

40% = 2/5 = 0.4
2

Convert 3/4 to a percentage

1

Divide: 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75

2

Multiply by 100: 0.75 × 100 = 75%

3/4 = 75%
3

Find 25% of 60

1

Method 1: 25% = 1/4, so 60 ÷ 4 = 15

2

Method 2: 0.25 × 60 = 15

25% of 60 = 15
4

A shirt costs $40 and is 10% off. What is the discount?

1

10% of $40 = 0.10 × 40 = $4

The discount is $4. Sale price = $36.
5

15 out of 20 students passed. What percentage passed?

1

Fraction: 15/20

2

15 ÷ 20 = 0.75 → 0.75 × 100 = 75%

75% of students passed.

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • DECIMAL 50% ≠ 0.5 sounds obvious, but 5% = 0.05 (move point two places left, not one).
  • OF = × "Percentage of" means multiply. 30% of 200 = 0.30 × 200, not 30 + 200.
  • 100% 100% of a number equals the whole number itself. 100% of 50 = 50.
  • SIMPLIFY When converting % to a fraction, always simplify: 60% = 60/100 = 3/5.
