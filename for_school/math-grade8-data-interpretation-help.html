Data Interpretation

Grade 8 Math  ·  Reading charts, pie graphs & drawing conclusions

📈 Common Chart Types

🥧
Pie Chart
Shows how a whole is divided into parts. Each sector represents a percentage or fraction.
📊
Bar Chart
Compares quantities across categories. Bar length = value.
📉
Line Graph
Shows change over time. Useful for trends and predictions.
🗋️
Frequency Table
Tallies how often each value occurs. Basis for most charts.

🥧 Pie Charts — Key Facts

Sector Angle
Angle = (frequency ÷ total) × 360°
Percentage from sector
% = (sector angle ÷ 360) × 100
Science — 40%
Maths — 30%
English — 20%
Art — 10%

The total of all percentages must equal 100%. The total of all angles must equal 360°.

✏️ Worked Examples

1

A pie chart shows 80 people chose Sport. Total = 200. What % is that?

1

% = (80 ÷ 200) × 100 = 40%

40% chose Sport.
2

A sector has an angle of 72°. What percentage does it represent?

1

% = (72 ÷ 360) × 100 = 20%

The sector represents 20%.
3

50 students prefer Maths out of 250. Find the sector angle for a pie chart.

1

Angle = (50 ÷ 250) × 360° = 0.2 × 360°

Sector angle = 72°
4

A bar chart shows sales: Mon=30, Tue=45, Wed=25. What is the mean daily sales?

1

Mean = (30 + 45 + 25) ÷ 3 = 100 ÷ 3

Mean ≈ 33.3 sales per day
5

A line graph shows temperatures rising from 12°C to 28°C over 8 weeks. What is the average weekly increase?

1

Total change = 28 − 12 = 16°C over 8 weeks.

2

Weekly increase = 16 ÷ 8 = 2°C

Average increase = 2°C per week

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • TOTALAlways identify the TOTAL before computing percentages or angles from a pie chart.
  • 360°All sector angles in a pie chart must sum to exactly 360°. Use this to find a missing angle.
  • SCALERead bar charts carefully — check what each grid line represents (the scale).
  • TRENDLine graphs show trends (direction of change), not exact values between plotted points.
