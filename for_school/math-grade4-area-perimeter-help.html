Area & Perimeter

Grade 4 Math  ·  Measure shapes with confidence

📏 What is Perimeter?

The perimeter is the total distance around the outside of a shape. Think of it as the length of a fence you would need to go all the way around a field.

Any shape
P = sum of all sides
Rectangle / Square
P = 2 × (l + w)

The answer is always in length units — cm, m, in, ft — never squared.

⬛ What is Area?

The area is the amount of flat space inside a shape. Think of it as how many unit squares fit inside.

Rectangle
A = length × width
Square
A = side × side = s²

The answer is always in square units — cm², m², in², ft². The little ² is essential!

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Find the perimeter and area of a rectangle 8 cm × 5 cm

1

Perimeter: P = 2 × (l + w) = 2 × (8 + 5) = 2 × 13

2

Area: A = l × w = 8 × 5

P = 26 cm    A = 40 cm²
2

A square has a side of 7 cm. Find P and A.

1

Perimeter: P = 4 × side = 4 × 7 = 28 cm

2

Area: A = 7 × 7 = 49 cm²

P = 28 cm    A = 49 cm²
3

A rectangle has perimeter 24 m and length 8 m. Find the width.

1

P = 2 × (l + w) → 24 = 2 × (8 + w)

2

Divide both sides by 2: 12 = 8 + w

3

w = 12 − 8 = 4 m

Width = 4 m
4

A garden is 9 m long and 6 m wide. How much fencing is needed?

1

Fencing = perimeter = 2 × (9 + 6) = 2 × 15

30 m of fencing
5

Rectangle A is 10 × 3. Rectangle B is 6 × 5. Which has the bigger area?

1

Area A = 10 × 3 = 30 cm²

2

Area B = 6 × 5 = 30 cm²

They are equal — both have area 30 cm²

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • UNITS Perimeter uses plain units (cm). Area uses squared units (cm²). Never mix them up!
  • ALL SIDES When finding perimeter of an irregular shape, add every side — don't skip any.
  • FORMULA For a rectangle, P = 2 × (l + w) — not just l + w. Multiply by 2 because there are two lengths and two widths.
  • SAME UNITS If one side is in cm and another in mm, convert to the same unit before calculating.
