Telling Time

Reading a clock is one of the most useful everyday skills you can learn! In this guide you will master the two hands of an analog clock, read times to the hour, half-hour, quarter-hour, and then in 5-minute steps.

Section 1

Parts of a Clock

An analog clock has a round face with the numbers 1 to 12. It has two hands:

🔴 Short hand (hour hand) — moves slowly; it points to the hour. It takes 1 full hour to move from one number to the next.

🔵 Long hand (minute hand) — moves faster; it goes all the way round the clock in 60 minutes. Each number it passes = 5 minutes.

Key positions of the minute hand:

• Points to 12 → 0 minutes (o'clock)  |  Points to 3 → 15 minutes (quarter past)
• Points to 6 → 30 minutes (half past)  |  Points to 9 → 45 minutes (quarter to)

Section 2

How to Read the Time

  1. 1
    Read the hour first. Look at the short hand. If it points directly at a number, that is the hour. If it is between two numbers, use the smaller number (the one it has just passed).
  2. 2
    Read the minutes. Look at the long hand. Count by 5s from 12 around to where the long hand points: 12→0, 1→5, 2→10, 3→15, 4→20, 5→25, 6→30, 7→35, 8→40, 9→45, 10→50, 11→55.
  3. 3
    Write the time. Put the hour before the colon and the minutes after: 3:15 (three fifteen). Always write two digits for minutes: 3:05, not 3:5.

Section 3

Worked Examples

Example 1 — O'clock (hour hand on a number, minute hand on 12)
The hour hand points to 4. The minute hand points to 12. What time is it?
Hour hand → 4 → hour is 4 points directly at 4
Minute hand → 12 → 0 minutes
✅ 4:00 (four o'clock)
Example 2 — Half past (minute hand on 6)
The hour hand is between 7 and 8. The minute hand points to 6. What time is it?
Hour hand between 7 and 8 → hour is 7 use the smaller number
Minute hand → 6 → count by 5s from 12: 5,10,15,20,25,30 → 30 minutes
✅ 7:30 (half past seven)
Example 3 — Quarter past (minute hand on 3)
The hour hand is just past 9. The minute hand points to 3. What time is it?
Hour hand just past 9 → hour is 9
Minute hand → 3 → 5, 10, 15 → 15 minutes
✅ 9:15 (quarter past nine)
Example 4 — Quarter to (minute hand on 9)
The hour hand is between 11 and 12. The minute hand points to 9. What time is it?
Hour hand between 11 and 12 → hour is 11
Minute hand → 9 → count by 5s from 12: 5,10,15,20,25,30,35,40,45 → 45 minutes
✅ 11:45 (quarter to twelve)
Example 5 — 5-minute interval
The hour hand is just past 2. The minute hand points to 5. What time is it?
Hour hand just past 2 → hour is 2
Minute hand → 5 → count by 5s: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25 → 25 minutes
✅ 2:25 (twenty-five past two)

Section 4

Watch Out! ⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Mixing up the hands: Using the long hand for hours
    ✅ Short hand = hours, Long hand = minutes. Remember: LONG minutes take longer to count!
  • ❌ Using the wrong hour when the hand is between two numbers: Saying 8 when the hour hand is between 7 and 8
    ✅ Always use the number the hour hand has ALREADY passed (the smaller one) — 7 in this case.
  • ❌ Counting each number as 1 minute: Thinking minute hand on 3 = 3 minutes
    ✅ Each number on the clock face = 5 minutes. Count by 5s from 12.
