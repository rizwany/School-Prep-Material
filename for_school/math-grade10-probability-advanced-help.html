P(E)

Probability (Events & Outcomes)

Grade 10 Math  ·  Classical probability, addition rule & independent events

🎲 Key Vocabulary

Experiment
A process with a well-defined set of outcomes (e.g., rolling a die).
Sample Space (S)
The set of ALL possible outcomes. n(S) = total outcomes.
Event (E)
Any subset of the sample space. n(E) = favourable outcomes.
Complementary Event
E′ = "E does NOT occur". P(E) + P(E′) = 1.
Mutually Exclusive
Events that CANNOT occur together. P(A ∩ B) = 0.
Independent Events
Occurrence of one does NOT affect the other. P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B).

📐 Probability Rules

Classical (Theoretical) Probability
P(E) = n(E) / n(S)
All outcomes equally likely. 0 ≤ P(E) ≤ 1 always.
Complementary Rule
P(E′) = 1 − P(E)
Use when "at least one" or "not" appears in the question.
Addition Rule (Mutually Exclusive)
P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B)
Only when A and B cannot happen at the same time.
Addition Rule (General)
P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A ∩ B)
Works for any two events. Subtract overlap to avoid double-counting.
Multiplication Rule (Independent)
P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B)
Only when A and B are independent (one doesn't affect the other).

✏️ Worked Examples

1

A fair die is rolled. Find P(even) and P(not even).

1

S = {1,2,3,4,5,6}, n(S) = 6. Even = {2,4,6}, n(E) = 3.

2

P(even) = 3/6 = 1/2. P(not even) = 1 − 1/2 = 1/2.

P(even) = 1/2, P(not even) = 1/2
2

A bag has 4 red and 6 blue balls. Two balls are drawn with replacement. Find P(both red).

1

P(red on one draw) = 4/10 = 2/5. Draws are independent (with replacement).

2

P(both red) = (2/5) × (2/5) = 4/25.

P(both red) = 4/25
3

P(A) = 0.4, P(B) = 0.3, A and B are mutually exclusive. Find P(A or B).

1

Mutually exclusive: P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) = 0.4 + 0.3 = 0.7.

P(A or B) = 0.7
4

P(A) = 0.5, P(B) = 0.4, P(A ∩ B) = 0.2. Find P(A ∪ B).

1

P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A ∩ B) = 0.5 + 0.4 − 0.2 = 0.7.

P(A ∪ B) = 0.7
5

A card is drawn from a 52-card deck. Find P(king or heart).

1

P(king) = 4/52, P(heart) = 13/52, P(king of hearts) = 1/52.

2

P(king or heart) = 4/52 + 13/52 − 1/52 = 16/52 = 4/13.

P(king or heart) = 4/13

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • 0 to 1Probability is always between 0 and 1 inclusive. A negative probability or one greater than 1 signals an error.
  • REPLACEMENTDrawing WITH replacement = independent events (multiply). Drawing WITHOUT replacement = dependent (probabilities change each draw).
  • MUTUALLY EXCLUSIVEMutually exclusive does NOT mean independent. Two events can be exclusive (can't both happen) but not independent.
  • OVERLAPWhen using P(A ∪ B), always subtract P(A ∩ B) unless events are mutually exclusive (where P(A ∩ B) = 0).
