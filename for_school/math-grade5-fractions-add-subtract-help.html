Adding & Subtracting Fractions

Grade 5 Math  ·  Same and different denominators

➕ Same Denominator (Easy Case)

When fractions have the same denominator, simply add or subtract the numerators. The denominator stays the same.

Adding
a/d + b/d = (a+b)/d
Subtracting
a/d − b/d = (a−b)/d

Example: 3/8 + 2/8 = 5/8  ·  7/10 − 3/10 = 4/10 = 2/5 (simplified)

Always simplify your answer to lowest terms if possible.

🔢 Different Denominators (LCD Method)

When fractions have different denominators, find the Least Common Denominator (LCD) first, then convert both fractions before adding or subtracting.

  • 1
    Find the LCD (smallest number both denominators divide into evenly).
  • 2
    Convert each fraction to an equivalent fraction with the LCD as denominator.
  • 3
    Add or subtract the numerators. Keep the LCD as denominator.
  • 4
    Simplify the result if possible.
Example — 1/4 + 1/6
LCD of 4 and 6 = 12 → 3/12 + 2/12 = 5/12

✏️ Worked Examples

1

3/5 + 1/5 (same denominator)

1

Same denominator (5) — add numerators: 3 + 1 = 4

3/5 + 1/5 = 4/5
2

5/6 − 1/6 (same denominator)

1

Same denominator (6) — subtract numerators: 5 − 1 = 4

2

Simplify: 4/6 = 2/3 (divide both by 2)

5/6 − 1/6 = 4/6 = 2/3
3

1/3 + 1/4 (different denominators)

1

LCD of 3 and 4 = 12

2

1/3 = 4/12 (multiply top and bottom by 4)  ·  1/4 = 3/12 (multiply by 3)

3

4/12 + 3/12 = 7/12

1/3 + 1/4 = 7/12
4

3/4 − 2/5 (different denominators)

1

LCD of 4 and 5 = 20

2

3/4 = 15/20  ·  2/5 = 8/20

3

15/20 − 8/20 = 7/20

3/4 − 2/5 = 7/20
5

Sam ate 2/6 of a pizza. Lily ate 1/3. Together, how much did they eat?

1

Simplify 2/6 = 1/3. Now both fractions have the same denominator.

2

1/3 + 1/3 = 2/3

They ate 2/3 of the pizza together.

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • DENOMINATOR Never add the denominators! 1/4 + 1/4 = 2/4, NOT 2/8. Only the numerators are added.
  • LCD FIRST With different denominators, find the LCD before adding. You cannot add 1/3 + 1/5 directly.
  • SIMPLIFY Always check if your answer can be simplified (reduced to lowest terms).
  • MIXED NUMBERS For mixed numbers like 2 1/2, convert to improper fractions first: 2 1/2 = 5/2.
