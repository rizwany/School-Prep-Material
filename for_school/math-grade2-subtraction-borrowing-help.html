Subtraction with Borrowing

Sometimes the top digit in the ones column is smaller than the bottom digit — so we can't subtract directly. Instead, we borrow a ten from the tens column and add it to the ones. This guide shows you every step clearly.

⏱ ~5 min read 🇵🇰 اردو 📝 Jump to Worksheet →

Section 1

What is Borrowing?

Borrowing (also called regrouping) happens when the ones digit of the top number is smaller than the ones digit of the bottom number, so you cannot subtract directly.

The solution: take 1 ten from the tens column, convert it into 10 ones, and add those 10 ones to the ones column. Now you can subtract!

Example: 72 − 35. Ones: 2 < 5, so borrow 1 ten → ones become 12. Now 12 − 5 = 7. Tens: 7−1 = 6 (after borrowing), 6 − 3 = 3. Answer: 37.

Section 2

How to Subtract with Borrowing

  1. 1
    Look at the ones column. Is the top digit smaller than the bottom digit? If yes, you need to borrow.
  2. 2
    Borrow from the tens column. Cross out the tens digit, reduce it by 1, and write the new smaller value above it.
  3. 3
    Add 10 to the ones digit. Write 1 in front of the ones digit (or add 10 to it). Now subtract the ones column.
  4. 4
    Subtract the tens column using the reduced tens digit.

Section 3

Worked Examples

Example 1 — 72 − 35
Work out 72 − 35.
Ones: 2 < 5 → borrow! top ones is smaller
Borrow 1 ten: tens 7→6, ones 2→12
Ones: 12 − 5 = 7
Tens: 6 − 3 = 3
✅ Answer: 37
Example 2 — 84 − 47
Work out 84 − 47.
Ones: 4 < 7 → borrow!
Borrow 1 ten: tens 8→7, ones 4→14
Ones: 14 − 7 = 7
Tens: 7 − 4 = 3
✅ Answer: 37
Example 3 — 63 − 28
Work out 63 − 28.
Ones: 3 < 8 → borrow!
Borrow 1 ten: tens 6→5, ones 3→13
Ones: 13 − 8 = 5
Tens: 5 − 2 = 3
✅ Answer: 35
Example 4 — 90 − 34 (tens digit is 0-like)
Work out 90 − 34.
Ones: 0 < 4 → borrow!
Borrow 1 ten: tens 9→8, ones 0→10
Ones: 10 − 4 = 6
Tens: 8 − 3 = 5
✅ Answer: 56
Example 5 — Checking with addition
Check: 72 − 35 = 37. Is that right?
Add back: 37 + 35 = 72? subtraction check
37 + 35: ones 7+5=12, write 2 carry 1; tens 3+3+1=7 → 72 ✓
✅ Correct — 37 + 35 = 72

Section 4

Watch Out! ⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Subtracting the smaller from the larger regardless of position: Writing 72−35 ones as 5−2=3
    ✅ You must always subtract bottom from top. If top < bottom, borrow first.
  • ❌ Forgetting to reduce the tens digit after borrowing: Using original tens digit
    ✅ After borrowing, the tens digit decreases by 1. Always update it before subtracting.
  • ❌ Borrowing when it isn't needed: Borrowing for 9 − 2 in the ones column
    ✅ Only borrow when the top ones digit is LESS than the bottom ones digit.

Section 5

Quick Reference

When to borrow
Top ones digit < bottom ones digit.
What borrowing does
Takes 1 ten → adds 10 to ones. Tens digit shrinks by 1.
Column order
Ones first, then tens (right to left).
Check
Add answer + smaller number = larger number.
Ones after borrow
Original ones digit + 10. E.g. 2 → 12.
Regrouping
1 ten = 10 ones. Borrowing converts between them.
