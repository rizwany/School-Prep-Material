Exponents & Powers

Grade 7  ·  Laws of exponents, zero & negative powers

Practice Worksheet →
Key Concepts
Base & Exponent
an

a = base, n = exponent (power). Means multiply a by itself n times.

Zero Power
a0 = 1

Any non-zero base raised to the power 0 equals 1. e.g. 70 = 1.

Negative Power
a−n

a−n = 1 / an. e.g. 2−3 = 1/8.

Squared & Cubed
² & ³

a2 = a × a (area). a3 = a × a × a (volume).

Laws of Exponents
Product Rule
am × an = am+n
e.g. 32 × 34 = 36 = 729
Quotient Rule
am ÷ an = am−n
e.g. 56 ÷ 52 = 54 = 625
Power of a Power
(am)n = am×n
e.g. (23)2 = 26 = 64
Power of a Product
(ab)n = an × bn
e.g. (2×3)2 = 4 × 9 = 36
Power of a Quotient
(a/b)n = an / bn
e.g. (2/3)2 = 4/9
Zero Exponent
a0 = 1 (a ≠ 0)
e.g. 1000 = 1, (−5)0 = 1
Worked Examples
1. Evaluate 25
25 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2
= 4 × 2 × 2 × 2 = 8 × 2 × 2 = 16 × 2
= 32
2. Simplify: 34 × 32
Same base → add exponents
34+2 = 36
= 729
3. Simplify: (23)4
Power of a power → multiply exponents
23×4 = 212
= 4096
4. Evaluate: 4−2
Negative exponent → reciprocal
4−2 = 1 / 42 = 1 / 16
= 0.0625
5. Write 8 000 000 in scientific notation
8 000 000 = 8 × 1 000 000
1 000 000 = 106
= 8 × 106
Remember: the laws of exponents only apply when the bases are the same. You cannot directly add exponents when bases differ (e.g. 23 × 32 must be evaluated separately).
