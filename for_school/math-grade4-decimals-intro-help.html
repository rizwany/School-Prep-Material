Decimals Intro
Tenths & Hundredths

Decimals let us write numbers between whole numbers. The decimal point separates the whole part from the fractional part. In Grade 4 we focus on tenths (one place after the dot) and hundredths (two places after the dot).

⏱ ~5 min read 📝 Jump to Worksheet →

Section 1

Place Value with Decimals

Every digit in a number has a place value. After the decimal point, the places are tenths and hundredths.

One tenth = 0.1 = 1/10  |  One hundredth = 0.01 = 1/100.

Section 2

Reading & Writing Decimals

How to say a decimal out loud:

  • 0.7 → "zero point seven" or "seven tenths"
  • 0.43 → "zero point four three" or "forty-three hundredths"
  • 1.25 → "one point two five" or "one and twenty-five hundredths"
  • 2.09 → "two point zero nine" or "two and nine hundredths" (note the 0 in tenths)

Section 3

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Decimal to fraction
Write 0.7 as a fraction.
0.7 = 7 tenths = 7/10
✅ Answer: 7/10
Example 2 — Hundredths decimal
Write 0.43 as a fraction.
0.43 = 43 hundredths = 43/100
✅ Answer: 43/100
Example 3 — Mixed decimal place value
In 2.58, which digit is in the tenths place?
2 . 5 8
Ones: 2   Tenths: 5   Hundredths: 8
✅ Answer: 5 is in the tenths place
Example 4 — Comparing decimals
Which is greater: 0.6 or 0.48?
Line up the decimal points:
  0.60
  0.48
Tenths place: 6 > 4, so 0.6 > 0.48 (even though 48 looks bigger as whole numbers)
✅ 0.6 is greater than 0.48
Example 5 — Ordering decimals
Order from smallest to largest: 0.3, 0.09, 0.25.
Write with same number of decimal places: 0.30, 0.09, 0.25
Compare: 0.09 < 0.25 < 0.30
✅ Smallest to largest: 0.09, 0.25, 0.3

Section 4

Watch Out! ⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Ignoring the decimal point when comparing: Thinking 0.48 > 0.6 because 48 > 6.
    ✅ Always compare digit by digit from the left after the decimal point. 0.6 = 0.60, and 60 > 48, so 0.6 > 0.48.
  • ❌ Forgetting a zero in the middle: Writing 2.9 when you mean 2.09 (two and nine hundredths).
    ✅ 2.09 has a 0 in the tenths place — that zero must be written. 2.9 means nine tenths, which is ten times bigger.
  • ❌ Adding trailing zeros changes the number: Thinking 0.3 ≠ 0.30.
    ✅ 0.30 = 0.3 (adding a zero at the end of the decimal does NOT change its value).

Section 5

Quick Reference

Tenths
First place after decimal point. 0.1 = 1/10. Ten tenths = 1 whole.
Hundredths
Second place after decimal point. 0.01 = 1/100. 100 hundredths = 1 whole.
Comparing
Line up decimal points, then compare digit by digit from the left.
Trailing zeros
0.5 = 0.50. Adding a zero after the last decimal digit does not change the value.
Middle zeros
0.09 ≠ 0.9. A zero between the decimal point and another digit changes the value.
Decimal → fraction
Count decimal places: 1 → /10; 2 → /100. e.g. 0.37 = 37/100.
