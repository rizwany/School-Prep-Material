Area of Triangles & Quadrilaterals

Grade 6 Math  ·  Calculating areas of flat shapes

📐 Area Formulas

Area is the amount of space inside a 2D shape. It is measured in square units (cm², m²).

Triangle

A = ½ × b × h

b = base, h = perpendicular height (not slant)

Rectangle / Square

A = l × w  (Rectangle)
A = s²  (Square)

l = length, w = width, s = side length

Parallelogram

A = b × h

h must be the perpendicular (vertical) height, not the slant side

Trapezium (Trapezoid)

A = ½ × (a + b) × h

a and b = the two parallel sides, h = perpendicular height

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Triangle with base = 10 cm, height = 6 cm

1

Use the formula: A = ½ × b × h

2

A = ½ × 10 × 6 = ½ × 60 = 30

Area = 30 cm²
2

Rectangle 8 cm × 5 cm

1

A = l × w = 8 × 5 = 40

Area = 40 cm²
3

Parallelogram with base = 12 cm, perpendicular height = 4 cm

1

A = b × h = 12 × 4 = 48

Area = 48 cm²
4

Trapezium with parallel sides 6 cm and 10 cm, height 4 cm

1

A = ½ × (a + b) × h

2

A = ½ × (6 + 10) × 4 = ½ × 16 × 4 = ½ × 64 = 32

Area = 32 cm²
5

A triangle has area 24 cm² and base 8 cm. Find the height.

1

A = ½ × b × h → 24 = ½ × 8 × h

2

24 = 4h → h = 24 ÷ 4 = 6

Height = 6 cm

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • PERPENDICULAR HEIGHT Always use the perpendicular (vertical) height, not the slant side length of a parallelogram or triangle.
  • HALF FOR TRIANGLE Don't forget the ½ in the triangle formula. A very common mistake is using just b × h.
  • UNITS² Area is always in square units. If sides are in cm, area is cm². If in m, area is m².
  • TRAPEZIUM In a trapezium, add BOTH parallel sides before multiplying by the height and ½.
