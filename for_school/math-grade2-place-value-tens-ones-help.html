Place Value — Tens & Ones

Every digit in a number holds a secret: its position tells you exactly how much it is worth. In this guide you will learn about the tens place and the ones place, write numbers in expanded form, and compare 2-digit numbers with confidence.

⏱ ~5 min read 🇵🇰 اردو 📝 Jump to Worksheet →

Section 1

What is Place Value?

Place value means that the position of a digit tells us how much it is worth. The same digit can have very different values depending on where it sits.

For 2-digit numbers we have two places:

📦 Tens place — the digit on the left tells us how many groups of ten.
📌 Ones place — the digit on the right tells us how many single units.

TensOnes
47

The number 47: the 4 is in the tens place (= 40) and the 7 is in the ones place (= 7).

Section 2

How to Find Tens and Ones

  1. 1
    Look at the left digit. That is the tens digit. Multiply it by 10 to find its value.
  2. 2
    Look at the right digit. That is the ones digit. Its face value is its place value.
  3. 3
    Add them together. Tens value + Ones value = the full number. This is called expanded form.
  4. 4
    Compare numbers by looking at the tens digit first. More tens = bigger number. If tens are equal, compare the ones digit.

Section 3

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Find tens and ones
How many tens and how many ones are in 63?
Tens digit: 6 → 6 × 10 = 60 left digit
Ones digit: 3 → 3 × 1 = 3 right digit
✅ 6 tens and 3 ones
Example 2 — Build a number from parts
What number has 5 tens and 2 ones?
5 tens = 50 5 × 10
2 ones = 2
50 + 2 = 52
✅ Answer: 52
Example 3 — Expanded form
Write 78 in expanded form.
Tens: 7 × 10 = 70
Ones: 8 × 1 = 8
78 = 70 + 8
✅ 78 = 70 + 8
Example 4 — From expanded form to number
What number is equal to 40 + 6?
40 = 4 tens, 6 = 6 ones
Tens digit: 4, Ones digit: 6 → write 46
✅ Answer: 46
Example 5 — Compare numbers
Which is greater: 35 or 53?
35: 3 tens; 53: 5 tens compare tens first
5 tens > 3 tens, so 53 > 35
✅ 53 is greater

Section 4

Watch Out! ⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Swapping tens and ones: Reading 54 as "5 ones and 4 tens"
    ✅ The LEFT digit is ALWAYS tens. 54 = 5 tens + 4 ones = 50 + 4.
  • ❌ Confusing the value with the digit: Saying "the 6 in 63 is worth 6"
    ✅ The 6 in 63 is in the tens place, so it is worth 60, not 6.
  • ❌ Comparing ones before tens: Thinking 39 > 41 because 9 > 1
    ✅ Always compare tens digits first. 41 has more tens, so 41 > 39.

Section 5

Quick Reference

Ones place

Comparing
