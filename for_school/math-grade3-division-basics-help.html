Division Basics

In this guide, you'll learn how to divide numbers by sharing them equally into groups — and how division and multiplication are two sides of the same coin.

⏱ ~5 min read 📝 Jump to Worksheet →

Section 1

What is Division?

Division means splitting a number into equal groups. When we write 12 ÷ 3 = 4, we're saying: "Split 12 into 3 equal groups — each group gets 4."

The number being split is called the dividend. The number of groups is the divisor. The answer is the quotient.

Division is the opposite (inverse) of multiplication. If 3 × 4 = 12, then 12 ÷ 3 = 4 and 12 ÷ 4 = 3.

Section 2

Step-by-Step Method

Dividend ÷ Divisor = Quotient
e.g.   20 ÷ 5 = 4
  1. 1
    Read the problem. Identify the dividend (total) and divisor (number of groups or group size). E.g. "24 ÷ 4 = ?"
  2. 2
    Use your times tables. Ask: "4 times WHAT equals 24?" Think through the 4 times table: 4×1=4, 4×2=8 … 4×6=24 ✓
  3. 3
    Write the quotient. Since 4 × 6 = 24, we know 24 ÷ 4 = 6.
  4. 4
    Check your answer. Multiply back: quotient × divisor = dividend. 6 × 4 = 24 ✓
Quotient × Divisor = Dividend
Use multiplication to check division

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Easy: Divide by 2
What is 14 ÷ 2?
14 ÷ 2 split 14 into 2 equal groups
Think: 2 × ? = 14
2 × 7 = 14 ✓
✅ Answer: 7
Example 2 — Easy: Divide by 10
What is 50 ÷ 10?
50 ÷ 10 multiples of 10 are easy to spot
10 × 5 = 50 ✓
✅ Answer: 5
Example 3 — Medium: Missing Dividend
? ÷ 6 = 7   What is the missing number?
We need: ? ÷ 6 = 7
Rearrange: ? = 7 × 6
= 42
✅ Answer: 42
Example 4 — Hard: Missing Divisor
63 ÷ ? = 9   What is the missing divisor?
63 ÷ ? = 9
Rearrange: ? = 63 ÷ 9
Think: 9 × 7 = 63
So ? = 7
✅ Answer: 7
Example 5 — Word Problem: Equal Sharing
32 children are split into 8 equal teams. How many children are on each team?
Total ÷ number of groups = group size
32 ÷ 8 = ?
Think: 8 × 4 = 32 ✓
✅ Answer: 4 children

Section 4

Watch Out! ⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Confusing dividend and divisor: Writing 3 ÷ 12 when you mean 12 ÷ 3
    ✅ The bigger number (total) is usually divided by the smaller number (groups): dividend ÷ divisor
  • ❌ Forgetting the link to multiplication: Trying to subtract repeatedly instead
    ✅ Use your times tables! Ask "? × divisor = dividend" — it's much faster
  • ❌ Thinking division is commutative: Believing 12 ÷ 3 = 3 ÷ 12
    ✅ Division is NOT commutative. 12 ÷ 3 = 4, but 3 ÷ 12 does not equal 4

Section 5

Quick Reference

Key Formula
Dividend ÷ Divisor = Quotient
Dividend
The total number being divided. E.g. in 12 ÷ 3, the dividend is 12.
Divisor
The number you divide by (number of groups). E.g. in 12 ÷ 3, the divisor is 3.
Quotient
The answer to a division problem. E.g. in 12 ÷ 3 = 4, the quotient is 4.
Inverse Operation
Division and multiplication are inverses. If 4×3=12 then 12÷3=4.
Equal Sharing
Dividing a total into equal groups. Each group gets the same amount.
Division by 1
Any number ÷ 1 = itself. E.g. 9 ÷ 1 = 9.
