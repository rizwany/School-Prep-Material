Multiplying Fractions

Grade 5 Math  ·  Multiply across top and bottom

📐 The Multiplication Rule

Multiplying fractions is simpler than adding them — no need for a common denominator! Simply multiply the numerators together and the denominators together.

Fraction × Fraction
(a/b) × (c/d) = (a×c) / (b×d)
Whole Number × Fraction
n × (a/b) = (n×a) / b

Example: 2/3 × 3/4 = (2×3)/(3×4) = 6/12 = 1/2

✂️ Cross-Cancelling (Simplify First)

You can simplify before multiplying by cancelling a numerator with a denominator on the other fraction. This keeps numbers smaller and avoids simplifying a big fraction at the end.

  • 1
    Look diagonally: can any numerator and denominator share a common factor?
  • 2
    Divide both by that factor (cancel).
  • 3
    Multiply the remaining numbers.
Cross-cancel example — 3/4 × 8/9
3 and 9 share factor 3: 3→1, 9→3. 4 and 8 share factor 4: 4→1, 8→2. Result: 1/1 × 2/3 = 2/3

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • NO LCD Unlike adding, you do NOT need a common denominator to multiply. Just multiply straight across.
  • SIMPLIFY Always simplify your final answer. 6/12 should become 1/2.
  • "OF" = × In word problems, "of" always means multiply. "2/3 of 15" = 2/3 × 15.
  • WHOLE NUMBERS Write whole numbers over 1 before multiplying: 5 = 5/1.
