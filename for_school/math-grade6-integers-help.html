Integers

Grade 6 Math  ·  Working with positive and negative numbers

📏 The Number Line

Integers are all whole numbers including zero and their negatives: … −3, −2, −1, 0, 1, 2, 3 …

Negative numbers are to the left of zero; positive numbers are to the right.

📐 Rules for Operations

Addition

  • (+) + (+) → positive
  • (−) + (−) → negative
  • (+) + (−) → sign of larger

Subtraction

  • Subtracting a negative = adding
  • a − (−b) = a + b
  • Move right on the number line

Multiplication

  • Same signs → positive
  • (+)×(+) = +   (−)×(−) = +
  • Different signs → negative

Division

  • Same signs → positive
  • (+)÷(+) = +   (−)÷(−) = +
  • Different signs → negative
Sign Rule (× and ÷)
Same signs → Positive  |  Different signs → Negative

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Calculate: (−5) + 3

1

Different signs — subtract the smaller absolute value from the larger: 5 − 3 = 2

2

Keep the sign of the number with the larger absolute value: −5 is larger, so the answer is negative.

(−5) + 3 = −2
2

Calculate: 4 − (−3)

1

Subtracting a negative is the same as adding: 4 − (−3) = 4 + 3

2

4 + 3 = 7

4 − (−3) = 7
3

Calculate: (−3) × (−4)

1

Multiply the numbers: 3 × 4 = 12

2

Same signs (both negative) → result is positive.

(−3) × (−4) = +12
4

Calculate: 15 ÷ (−3)

1

Divide the numbers: 15 ÷ 3 = 5

2

Different signs (positive ÷ negative) → result is negative.

15 ÷ (−3) = −5
5

The temperature was −4°C and dropped 6 more degrees. What is the new temperature?

1

"Dropped 6 degrees" means subtract 6: −4 − 6

2

Both values are being taken away from zero: −4 − 6 = −10

New temperature = −10°C

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • DOUBLE NEGATIVE Subtracting a negative equals adding a positive. Don't forget: a − (−b) = a + b.
  • SIGN RULES The sign rules (same → positive, different → negative) only apply to multiplication and division, NOT addition.
  • ABSOLUTE VALUE When adding integers with different signs, subtract their absolute values and use the sign of the larger absolute value.
  • NUMBER LINE On a number line, −10 is less than −2 even though 10 > 2. Further left = smaller number.
