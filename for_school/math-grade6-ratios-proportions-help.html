Ratios & Proportions

Grade 6 Math  ·  Comparing quantities and solving proportions

⚖️ What is a Ratio?

A ratio compares two quantities of the same kind. It tells you how much of one thing there is compared to another.

A ratio can be written in three ways:

Colon Notation

3 : 4 — read as "3 to 4"

Fraction Notation

3/4 — ratio as a fraction

Word Form

"3 to 4" — plain English

Simplifying

Divide both parts by their GCF: 6:9 = 2:3

🔗 Proportions

A proportion is a statement that two ratios are equal: a/b = c/d

To solve a proportion, use cross multiplication: multiply the diagonals and set them equal.

Cross Multiplication Rule
a/b = c/d  →  a × d = b × c
Simplifying a Ratio
Divide both terms by their Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Simplify the ratio 12 : 18

1

Find the GCF of 12 and 18. GCF = 6

2

Divide both terms: 12 ÷ 6 = 2,   18 ÷ 6 = 3

12 : 18 = 2 : 3
2

Write the ratio of 15 boys to 25 girls in simplest form

1

Ratio = 15 : 25. Find GCF = 5

2

15 ÷ 5 = 3,   25 ÷ 5 = 5

Boys : Girls = 3 : 5
3

Solve for x: x/4 = 6/8

1

Cross multiply: x × 8 = 4 × 6

2

8x = 24 → x = 24 ÷ 8 = 3

x = 3
4

A recipe needs 2 cups of flour for every 3 cookies. How many cups for 12 cookies?

1

Set up proportion: 2/3 = x/12

2

Cross multiply: 3x = 2 × 12 = 24

3

x = 24 ÷ 3 = 8

8 cups of flour needed
5

Are 3:4 and 9:12 equivalent ratios?

1

Cross multiply: 3 × 12 = 36   and   4 × 9 = 36

2

Both products are equal (36 = 36), so the ratios are equivalent.

Yes — 3:4 and 9:12 are equivalent ratios

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • ORDER MATTERS 3:4 is NOT the same as 4:3. Always write ratios in the order the question asks.
  • SAME UNITS Both quantities must be in the same unit before writing a ratio. Convert first if needed.
  • SIMPLIFY Always check if a ratio can be simplified. Divide both parts by their GCF.
  • CROSS MULTIPLY When solving proportions, make sure you multiply diagonals correctly: top-left × bottom-right = top-right × bottom-left.
