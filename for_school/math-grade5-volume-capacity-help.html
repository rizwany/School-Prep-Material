Volume & Capacity

Grade 5 Math  ·  Measure 3D space and liquid amounts

📦 What is Volume?

Volume is the amount of 3D space a solid object occupies — how many unit cubes fit inside it. Volume is measured in cubic units: cm³, m³, or in³.

Rectangular Prism (Cuboid)
V = length × width × height
Cube
V = side × side × side = s³

🧴 What is Capacity?

Capacity is the maximum amount of liquid a container can hold. It is measured in litres (L) and millilitres (mL).

Conversion
1 L = 1000 mL
Volume ↔ Capacity
1 cm³ = 1 mL

So a box with volume 500 cm³ can hold 500 mL = 0.5 L of liquid.

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Find the volume of a box 6 cm × 4 cm × 3 cm

1

V = l × w × h = 6 × 4 × 3

2

6 × 4 = 24; 24 × 3 = 72

Volume = 72 cm³
2

A cube has a side of 5 cm. Find its volume.

1

V = s³ = 5 × 5 × 5

2

5 × 5 = 25; 25 × 5 = 125

Volume = 125 cm³
3

A container holds 3.5 L. How many mL is that?

1

1 L = 1000 mL, so 3.5 L = 3.5 × 1000

3.5 L = 3500 mL
4

A fish tank is 40 cm × 20 cm × 25 cm. What is its capacity in litres?

1

Volume = 40 × 20 × 25 = 20 000 cm³

2

1 cm³ = 1 mL, so 20 000 cm³ = 20 000 mL = 20 L

Capacity = 20 L
5

A box has volume 120 cm³. Its length is 6 cm and width is 4 cm. Find the height.

1

V = l × w × h → 120 = 6 × 4 × h = 24h

2

h = 120 ÷ 24 = 5

Height = 5 cm

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • CUBIC UNITS Volume uses cubic units (cm³ or m³), not square units (cm²) — that's for area.
  • SAME UNITS Make sure all three dimensions are in the same unit before multiplying.
  • 1 cm³ = 1 mL This handy fact converts volume of a box into liquid capacity.
  • ORDER Multiplication is commutative: l × w × h = h × l × w. You can multiply in any order.
