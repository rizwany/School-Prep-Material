2D & 3D Shapes

Shapes are everywhere! In this guide you will learn about flat 2D shapes — their sides and corners — and solid 3D shapes — their faces, edges, and vertices. You'll also spot these shapes in the real world around you.

⏱ ~6 min read 📝 Jump to Worksheet →

Section 1

2D Shapes — Flat Shapes

2D shapes are flat. They have only length and width — no thickness. We describe them by counting their sides and corners (also called vertices).

ShapeSymbolSidesCornersReal-world example
Circle⭕0 (curved)0Pizza, wheel, coin
Triangle🔺33Yield sign, roof peak, slice
Square🟦4 (equal)4Tile, chessboard square
Rectangle▬44Door, book, TV screen
Pentagon⬠55Home plate (baseball), sign
Hexagon⬡66Honeycomb cell, bolt head

💡 Square vs Rectangle: A square is a special rectangle where ALL four sides are equal. Every square is a rectangle, but not every rectangle is a square.

Section 2

3D Shapes — Solid Shapes

3D shapes are solid — they have length, width, and height. We describe them using:

• Faces — flat or curved surfaces
• Edges — where two faces meet (a line)
• Vertices — corners where edges meet (a point)

ShapeFlat FacesEdgesVerticesReal-world example
Sphere 🌐000Ball, orange, globe
Cube 🎲6 (squares)128Dice, ice cube, building block
Cuboid (box) 📦6 (rectangles)128Cereal box, shoe box, brick
Cylinder 🥫2 circles2 curved0Can, tube, glass
Cone 🍦1 circle1 curved1 (tip)Ice cream cone, party hat, funnel
Pyramid 🔺5 (1 square + 4 triangles)85Egyptian pyramids, tent

Section 3

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Identify a 2D shape by its sides
A shape has 6 sides and 6 corners. What is it called?
6 sides → hexagon check the 2D table
✅ Hexagon
Example 2 — Count faces of a cube
How many flat faces does a cube have?
Top, bottom, front, back, left, right = 6 faces
✅ 6 flat faces
Example 3 — Real-world shape
A tin of soup is what 3D shape?
Round top and bottom + curved side = cylinder
✅ Cylinder
Example 4 — Edges of a cube
How many edges does a cube have?
4 edges on top + 4 on bottom + 4 connecting = 12 edges
✅ 12 edges
Example 5 — 2D vs 3D
Is a circle the same as a sphere?
Circle = 2D flat shape (like drawn on paper)
Sphere = 3D solid shape (like a ball)
✅ No — circle is 2D, sphere is 3D

Section 4

Watch Out! ⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Confusing 2D and 3D names: Calling a sphere a "circle"
    ✅ Circle = flat drawing, Sphere = solid ball. A square is 2D; a cube is 3D.
  • ❌ Saying a square is NOT a rectangle: "It can't be both"
    ✅ A square IS a rectangle — it just has all four sides equal.
  • ❌ Counting curved edges as straight edges: Saying a cylinder has 2 flat edges
    ✅ Cylinder edges are curved. It has 0 straight edges and 2 curved edges where the flat circles meet the curved surface.
