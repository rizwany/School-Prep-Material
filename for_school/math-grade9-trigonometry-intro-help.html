Introduction to Trigonometry

Grade 9 Math  ·  Right-triangle ratios, SOH-CAH-TOA & standard angles

📐 The Three Trigonometric Ratios

In a right-angled triangle, for a given angle θ:

sin θ
Opposite / Hypotenuse
SOH
cos θ
Adjacent / Hypotenuse
CAH
tan θ
Opposite / Adjacent
TOA

Memory trick: SOH-CAH-TOA — pronounce it like one word.

The hypotenuse is always the longest side, opposite the right angle. Opposite and adjacent depend on which angle θ you are using.

🔍 Key Identity

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

This holds for any angle θ. It comes directly from the Pythagorean theorem: (opp)² + (adj)² = (hyp)², divided by hyp².

Also useful: tan θ = sin θ / cos θ

✏️ Worked Examples

1

In a right triangle, opposite = 3, hypotenuse = 5. Find sin θ, cos θ, tan θ.

1

Adjacent = √(5² − 3²) = √(25 − 9) = √16 = 4 (Pythagoras).

2

sin θ = 3/5, cos θ = 4/5, tan θ = 3/4.

sin θ = 0.6, cos θ = 0.8, tan θ = 0.75
2

Find the value of sin 30° + cos 60°

1

From the standard table: sin 30° = ½, cos 60° = ½.

2

½ + ½ = 1.

sin 30° + cos 60° = 1
3

If sin θ = 5/13, find cos θ using the identity

1

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 → cos²θ = 1 − (5/13)² = 1 − 25/169 = 144/169.

2

cos θ = √(144/169) = 12/13.

cos θ = 12/13
4

A ladder leans against a wall. Angle with ground = 60°, ladder length = 10 m. Find height.

1

Height = opposite, ladder = hypotenuse. Use sin 60° = opp/hyp.

2

Height = 10 × sin 60° = 10 × (√3/2) = 5√3 ≈ 8.66 m.

Height ≈ 8.66 m (= 5√3 m)
5

Prove: tan²θ + 1 = sec²θ (where sec θ = 1/cos θ)

1

Start from sin²θ + cos²θ = 1. Divide every term by cos²θ.

2

sin²θ/cos²θ + 1 = 1/cos²θ → tan²θ + 1 = sec²θ. ✓

tan²θ + 1 = sec²θ — proved

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • RIGHT ∠These ratios only apply in right-angled triangles. For non-right triangles, use the sine/cosine rules.
  • OPPOSITEOpposite and adjacent sides depend on WHICH angle you label as θ. Relabelling changes the ratio.
  • tan 90°tan 90° is undefined because cos 90° = 0 (division by zero).
  • HYPOTENUSEThe hypotenuse is always opposite the right angle — it never changes regardless of which angle is θ.
