Mensuration

Grade 8 Math  ·  Surface area and volume of 3D shapes

📐 What is Mensuration?

Mensuration is the branch of mathematics dealing with the measurement of geometric figures — their lengths, areas, and volumes. At Grade 8 we focus on surface area (total outer area) and volume (space inside) of 3D solids.

Use π ≈ 3.14 or the π key on your calculator unless told otherwise.

📏 Surface Area & Volume Formulae

📦 Cuboid
SA = 2(lb + bh + lh)
V = l × b × h
🟦 Cube
SA = 6s²
V = s³
🛢️ Cylinder
SA = 2πr² + 2πrh
V = πr²h
🔺 Cone
SA = πr² + πrl
V = ⅓πr²h
⚽ Sphere
SA = 4πr²
V = ⁴⁄₃πr³
△ Triangular Prism
SA = 2(½bh) + perimeter×l
V = ½bh × l

In the cone formula, l is the slant height. For a right cone: l = √(r² + h²)

1

1

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • RADIUSDiameter is twice the radius. Always halve the diameter before substituting into formulae.
  • UNITSSurface area uses cm² (square units). Volume uses cm³ (cubic units). Don't mix them.
  • SLANTIn the cone formula, l is the slant height, NOT the vertical height h.
  • πUse 3.14 or 22/7 only when instructed. Otherwise leave answers in terms of π or use a calculator.
