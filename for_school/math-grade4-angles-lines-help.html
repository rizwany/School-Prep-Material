Angles & Lines

Grade 4 Math  ·  Identify and classify angles and lines

📐 What is an Angle?

An angle is the amount of turn between two rays that share the same starting point (the vertex). Angles are measured in degrees (°).

A full turn is 360°. A half turn (straight line) is 180°. A quarter turn (right angle) is 90°.

〰️ Types of Lines

Lines can relate to each other in different ways. Here are three important relationships:

Parallel Lines

Never meet. Always the same distance apart. Like railway tracks.

Perpendicular Lines

Meet at exactly 90°. Like the corner of a room or a plus sign.

Intersecting Lines

Cross each other at any angle (not necessarily 90°).

1

1

2

5

Railway tracks run side by side and never cross. What type of lines are they?

1

Lines that never meet and are always the same distance apart are parallel.

Parallel lines

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • 90° A right angle is exactly 90°, not "about" 90°. Look for the little square symbol in diagrams.
  • OBTUSE Obtuse angles look wide open. If an angle looks wider than the corner of a book, it's obtuse.
  • 180° Angles on a straight line always add to 180°. Use this to find a missing angle.
  • PARALLEL Parallel lines never touch — even if you extended them forever. Don't confuse them with lines that are merely far apart.
