Triangles (Congruence)

Grade 9 Math  ·  Congruence criteria, properties & similarity

≡ Congruent Triangles

Two triangles are congruent (≅) if they are identical in shape and size — all corresponding sides and angles are equal. The symbol ≅ means "is congruent to".

You only need to prove ONE of the criteria below — not all of them.

SSS
Side-Side-Side
All three corresponding sides are equal
SAS
Side-Angle-Side
Two sides and the included angle are equal
ASA
Angle-Side-Angle
Two angles and the included side are equal
AAS
Angle-Angle-Side
Two angles and a non-included side are equal
RHS
Right-Hypotenuse-Side
Right angle + hypotenuse + one side (right triangles only)

∼ Similar Triangles

Two triangles are similar (~) if they have the same shape but may differ in size. Corresponding angles are equal; corresponding sides are in the same ratio.

AA
Angle-Angle
Two pairs of corresponding angles are equal (third follows automatically)
SSS~
Side-Side-Side (ratio)
All three pairs of sides are in the same ratio
SAS~
Side-Angle-Side (ratio)
Two pairs of sides in the same ratio with the included angle equal

📌 Key Triangle Properties

Angle sum: The three interior angles of any triangle always sum to 180°.

Exterior angle: An exterior angle of a triangle equals the sum of the two non-adjacent interior angles.

Isosceles triangle: Base angles are equal. If two sides are equal, the angles opposite those sides are equal.

Equilateral triangle: All sides equal; all angles = 60°.

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Angles in △ABC are 50° and 70°. Find the third angle.

1

Angle sum = 180°: third angle = 180° − 50° − 70° = 60°.

Third angle = 60°
2

Which congruence criterion? △ABC: AB = 5, BC = 7, ∠B = 40°. △DEF: DE = 5, EF = 7, ∠E = 40°.

1

Two sides (5 and 7) and the angle BETWEEN them (40°) are equal.

2

This matches Side-Angle-Side.

△ABC ≅ △DEF by SAS
3

Exterior angle of a triangle is 110°. One non-adjacent interior angle is 65°. Find the other.

1

Exterior angle = sum of two non-adjacent interior angles.

2

Other angle = 110° − 65° = 45°.

Other interior angle = 45°
4

△PQR ~ △XYZ. PQ = 6, QR = 9, XY = 4. Find YZ.

1

Similar triangles → sides in proportion: PQ/XY = QR/YZ.

2

6/4 = 9/YZ → YZ = 9 × 4/6 = 6.

YZ = 6
5

Isosceles △ABC with AB = AC. ∠ABC = 55°. Find ∠BAC.

1

Isosceles → base angles equal: ∠ABC = ∠ACB = 55°.

2

∠BAC = 180° − 55° − 55° = 70°.

∠BAC = 70°

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • INCLUDEDIn SAS, the angle must be BETWEEN the two given sides. An angle not between the sides does NOT give SAS.
  • AAA ≠ ≅Three equal angles (AAA) proves similarity, NOT congruence. The triangles could be different sizes.
  • ORDERWhen writing congruence/similarity, the vertex order matters: △ABC ≅ △DEF means A↔D, B↔E, C↔F.
  • RHSRHS only applies to right-angled triangles. Confirm the right angle first.
