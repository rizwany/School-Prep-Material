Addition with Carrying

When you add two numbers and the ones column adds up to 10 or more, you need to carry the extra ten across to the tens column. This guide walks you through every step with clear worked examples.

⏱ ~5 min read 🇵🇰 اردو 📝 Jump to Worksheet →

Section 1

What is Carrying?

Carrying (also called regrouping) happens when the digits in a column add up to 10 or more.

Because our number system works in groups of ten, once you have 10 or more ones you can group them into a ten and move that ten to the tens column — that is the "carry".

Example: 7 + 8 = 15 ones → that's 1 ten and 5 ones. Write the 5 in the ones column and carry the 1 ten to the tens column.

Section 2

How to Add with Carrying

  1. 1
    Write the numbers in columns. Ones under ones, tens under tens. Always line up the right-hand digits.
  2. 2
    Add the ones column first. If the sum is less than 10, write it below the line. If it is 10 or more, write the ones digit only and carry the 1 to the top of the tens column.
  3. 3
    Add the tens column — including the carried 1. Write the result below the line.
  4. 4
    Check your answer by adding the two numbers in a different order (addition is commutative).

Section 3

Worked Examples

Example 1 — 37 + 45
Work out 37 + 45.
Ones: 7 + 5 = 12 → write 2, carry 1 12 ≥ 10
Tens: 3 + 4 + 1(carry) = 8
✅ Answer: 82
Example 2 — 56 + 28
Work out 56 + 28.
Ones: 6 + 8 = 14 → write 4, carry 1 14 ≥ 10
Tens: 5 + 2 + 1(carry) = 8
✅ Answer: 84
Example 3 — 47 + 36
Work out 47 + 36.
Ones: 7 + 6 = 13 → write 3, carry 1
Tens: 4 + 3 + 1(carry) = 8
✅ Answer: 83
Example 4 — 65 + 18
Work out 65 + 18.
Ones: 5 + 8 = 13 → write 3, carry 1
Tens: 6 + 1 + 1(carry) = 8
✅ Answer: 83
Example 5 — No carry needed (comparison)
Work out 23 + 14. Does it need carrying?
Ones: 3 + 4 = 7 → write 7 7 < 10, no carry!
Tens: 2 + 1 = 3
✅ Answer: 37 — no carry needed

Section 4

Watch Out! ⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Forgetting the carried 1: 37 + 45: adding 3+4=7 and writing 72
    ✅ Always add the carry to the tens: 3+4+1(carry)=8 → answer is 82.
  • ❌ Starting from the tens column: Working left to right like reading
    ✅ Always start with the ONES column first so you know if there is a carry.
  • ❌ Carrying the whole two-digit sum: Writing 12 below the ones column
    ✅ Write only the ones digit (2) below the line and carry just the 1.

Section 5

Quick Reference

When to carry
Ones column sum ≥ 10 → carry a 1 to tens.
What to carry
Always carry 1 (one group of ten), never more.
Column order
Ones first, then tens (right to left).
Check
Swap the numbers: 37+45 = 45+37. Same answer?
Carried 1 position
Write the small 1 above the tens column to remember it.
Regrouping
10 ones = 1 ten. That's all carrying really is.
