Time & Calendar

In this guide, you'll learn how to read analogue and digital clocks, tell the time to 5-minute intervals, and navigate calendars using days, weeks, and months.

⏱ ~5 min read 📝 Jump to Worksheet →

Section 1

Reading a Clock

A clock face has 12 numbers and two hands. The short (hour) hand points to the hour. The long (minute) hand points to the minutes.

Each number on the clock represents 5 minutes. So the 12 = 0 minutes, the 3 = 15 minutes, the 6 = 30 minutes, and the 9 = 45 minutes.

We can write time in two ways: analogue (clock face) or digital (e.g. 3:45 means 3 hours and 45 minutes).

Hour hand
3:00
Half past
6:30
Quarter past
4:15

Dark hand = hour, Blue hand = minutes. Minute hand at 12 = :00, at 6 = :30, at 3 = :15, at 9 = :45

Section 2

Step-by-Step Method

Hour : Minutes
Short hand → hour  |  Long hand → minute (×5 for each number)
  1. 1
    Read the hour first. Look at the short hand. It points near a number — that's the hour (use the smaller of the two numbers it's between).
  2. 2
    Read the minutes. Look at the long hand. Multiply the number it points to by 5. E.g. pointing at 7 → 7 × 5 = 35 minutes.
  3. 3
    Write as digital time. Write the hour, a colon, then two-digit minutes. E.g. 2 hours 35 minutes = 2:35.
  4. 4
    Elapsed time. To find how much time has passed, count forward in hours, then minutes. E.g. from 2:15 to 3:45 = 1 hour 30 minutes.
60 min = 1 hr  |  24 hrs = 1 day  |  7 days = 1 week

Section 3

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Reading Time (Hour)
The short hand points to 8. The long hand points to 12. What time is it?
Short hand → 8 o'clock (hour)
Long hand at 12 → 0 minutes
Time = 8:00
✅ Answer: 8:00 (eight o'clock)
Example 2 — Reading Time (Half Past)
The short hand is between 3 and 4. The long hand points to 6. What time is it?
Short hand between 3 and 4 → hour = 3
Long hand at 6 → 6 × 5 = 30 minutes
Time = 3:30
✅ Answer: 3:30 (half past three)
Example 3 — Reading Time (5-Minute Intervals)
Long hand points to 9. Short hand is just past 11. What time is it?
Long hand at 9 → 9 × 5 = 45 minutes
Short hand just past 11 → hour = 11
Time = 11:45 (quarter to 12)
✅ Answer: 11:45
Example 4 — Elapsed Time
School starts at 8:30 and ends at 2:30. How long is the school day?
From 8:30 to 2:30
Count hours: 8:30 → 9:30 → 10:30 → 11:30 → 12:30 → 1:30 → 2:30
= 6 hours
✅ Answer: 6 hours
Example 5 — Calendar Question
Today is Wednesday. What day will it be in 5 days?
Wed → Thu → Fri → Sat → Sun → Mon
5 days after Wednesday = Monday
✅ Answer: Monday

Section 4

Watch Out! ⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Mixing up the hands: Reading the long hand as hours and short hand as minutes
    ✅ SHORT hand = hours (it's "short" on time!). LONG hand = minutes.
  • ❌ Forgetting to multiply by 5: Saying the time is 7:7 when the minute hand is on 7
    ✅ Always multiply the number the minute hand points to by 5: 7 × 5 = 35, so it's :35
  • ❌ Off-by-one on elapsed time: Counting 8:00 to 11:00 as 2 hours instead of 3
    ✅ Count the jumps between hours, not the hours themselves: 8→9, 9→10, 10→11 = 3 jumps = 3 hours
