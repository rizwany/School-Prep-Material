Lines & Angles

Grade 9 Math  ·  Angle types, parallel lines & transversals

📐 Types of Angles

Acute
0° < θ < 90°
Smaller than a right angle
Right
θ = 90°
Exactly one quarter-turn
Obtuse
90° < θ < 180°
Between a right angle and a straight line
Straight
θ = 180°
A flat, straight line
Reflex
180° < θ < 360°
More than a straight angle
Complete
θ = 360°
Full rotation

🔄 Angle Pairs

Complementary
∠A + ∠B = 90°
Two angles that sum to 90°
Supplementary
∠A + ∠B = 180°
Two angles that sum to 180°
Linear Pair
Adjacent; sum = 180°
Adjacent angles on a straight line
Vertically Opposite
∠A = ∠C (opposite)
Formed by two intersecting lines — always equal

∥ Parallel Lines & Transversal

When a transversal crosses two parallel lines, special angle pairs are formed:

Corresponding Angles
Equal (F-shape)
∠1 = ∠5, ∠2 = ∠6, etc.
Alternate Interior Angles
Equal (Z-shape)
∠3 = ∠6, ∠4 = ∠5
Alternate Exterior Angles
Equal (Z-shape outside)
∠1 = ∠8, ∠2 = ∠7
Co-interior (Same-side)
Sum = 180° (C-shape)
∠3 + ∠5 = 180°

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Find the complement of 34°

1

Complementary angles sum to 90°.

2

Complement = 90° − 34° = 56°.

Complement = 56°
2

Two lines intersect. One angle is 115°. Find the other three angles.

1

Vertically opposite to 115° → 115°.

2

Linear pair with 115°: 180° − 115° = 65°.

3

Vertically opposite to 65° → 65°.

Angles: 115°, 65°, 115°, 65°
3

Parallel lines cut by transversal. Corresponding angle = 72°. Find alternate interior angle.

1

Corresponding angles are equal: the angle at the same position on the other parallel line = 72°.

2

Alternate interior angles are also equal to corresponding angles in this configuration = 72°.

Alternate interior angle = 72°
4

Co-interior angles are (3x + 10)° and (2x − 5)°. Find x.

1

Co-interior angles sum to 180°: (3x + 10) + (2x − 5) = 180.

2

5x + 5 = 180 → 5x = 175 → x = 35.

x = 35 (angles: 115° and 65°)
5

Angles at a point are 2x°, 3x°, and 4x°. Find each angle.

1

Angles at a point sum to 360°: 2x + 3x + 4x = 360.

2

9x = 360 → x = 40.

3

Angles: 80°, 120°, 160°.

80°, 120°, 160°

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • PARALLELCorresponding and alternate angle rules ONLY apply when lines are parallel. Always confirm parallelism first.
  • CO-INTERIORCo-interior angles (same-side interior) are supplementary (sum to 180°), NOT equal.
  • LINEAR PAIRA linear pair must be adjacent AND on a straight line. Not all supplementary angles form a linear pair.
  • VERT. OPP.Vertically opposite angles are always equal — you don't need to know if lines are parallel for this.
