Quadratic Expressions

Grade 8 Math  ·  Expanding and factorising

📐 What is a Quadratic Expression?

A quadratic expression contains an x² term as its highest power. The general form is:

Standard Form
ax² + bx + c  (where a ≠ 0)

Examples: x² + 5x + 6,  2x² − 3x + 1,  x² − 9

➕ Expanding (FOIL method)

To expand (x + a)(x + b), multiply every term in the first bracket by every term in the second — use the FOIL rule:

F
First
x · x = x²
O
Outer
x · b
I
Inner
a · x
L
Last
a · b
Result
(x + a)(x + b) = x² + (a+b)x + ab

🧩 Factorising (reverse of expanding)

To factorise x² + bx + c, find two numbers that multiply to c and add to b.

Difference of Squares
a² − b² = (a + b)(a − b)
Perfect Square
(x + a)² = x² + 2ax + a²

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Expand (x + 3)(x + 4)

1

F: x · x = x²

2

O + I: 4x + 3x = 7x

3

L: 3 × 4 = 12

(x + 3)(x + 4) = x² + 7x + 12
2

Expand (x − 5)(x + 2)

1

F: x²;   O: +2x;   I: −5x;   L: −10

2

Combine like terms: 2x − 5x = −3x

(x − 5)(x + 2) = x² − 3x − 10
3

Factorise x² + 7x + 12

1

Find two numbers that multiply to 12 and add to 7.

2

3 × 4 = 12 ✓ and 3 + 4 = 7 ✓

x² + 7x + 12 = (x + 3)(x + 4)
4

Factorise x² − 16 (difference of squares)

1

Recognise a² − b²: x² − 4² with b = 4.

2

Apply formula: (x + 4)(x − 4)

x² − 16 = (x + 4)(x − 4)
5

Expand (x + 5)²

1

Use perfect square formula: (x + a)² = x² + 2ax + a²

2

a = 5: x² + 2(5)x + 25 = x² + 10x + 25

(x + 5)² = x² + 10x + 25

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • MIDDLE TERMDon't forget the middle term when squaring: (x+3)² ≠ x²+9. It's x²+6x+9.
  • SIGNSWhen multiplying negatives: (x−3)(x−4) gives +12 as the constant (−3 × −4 = +12).
  • CHECKAlways expand your factorised answer to verify it matches the original expression.
  • DIFF OF SQDifference of squares only works when there is NO middle term: a²−b².
