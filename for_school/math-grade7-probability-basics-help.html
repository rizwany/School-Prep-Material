Probability Basics

Grade 7  ·  Probability scale, theoretical & experimental probability

Practice Worksheet →
The Probability Scale
0 (Impossible)0.5 (Even chance)1 (Certain)

Probability is always a number between 0 and 1 (inclusive). It can be written as a fraction, decimal, or percentage.

Key Concepts
Probability Formula
P(E) = f/n

f = favourable outcomes, n = total equally likely outcomes.

Complementary Events
P(E') = 1 − P(E)

P(event NOT happening) = 1 − P(event happening).

Sum of Probabilities
Sum = 1

All possible outcomes of an experiment must total 1.

Experimental Probability
P = freq / total

Based on actual results from an experiment or observation.

Important Vocabulary
Experiment
An action whose result is uncertain (e.g. rolling a die).
Outcome
A single possible result of an experiment.
Sample Space (S)
The set of ALL possible outcomes. e.g. S = {1,2,3,4,5,6} for a die.
Event
One or more outcomes of interest (subset of sample space).
Favourable Outcome
An outcome that satisfies the condition of the event.
Equally Likely
Each outcome has the same chance of occurring.
Worked Examples
1. A fair die is rolled. Find P(rolling a 4).
Sample space = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6} → n = 6
Favourable outcomes = {4} → f = 1
P(4) = 1/6
P(rolling a 4) = 1/6 ≈ 0.167
2. A bag has 3 red, 5 blue, and 2 green balls. Find P(picking blue).
Total balls = 3 + 5 + 2 = 10
Blue balls = 5
P(blue) = 5/10 = 1/2
P(blue) = 1/2 = 0.5
3. If P(rain tomorrow) = 0.3, what is P(no rain)?
Complementary rule: P(no rain) = 1 − P(rain)
= 1 − 0.3
P(no rain) = 0.7
4. A coin is tossed 50 times and lands heads 32 times. Find experimental P(heads).
Experimental probability = frequency / total trials
= 32 / 50 = 16/25
P(heads) = 16/25 = 0.64
5. A die is rolled. Find P(even number).
Even numbers in {1,2,3,4,5,6} = {2, 4, 6} → f = 3
P(even) = 3/6 = 1/2
P(even) = 1/2
Theoretical probability assumes all outcomes are equally likely. Experimental probability is calculated from real data — the more trials you do, the closer experimental probability gets to the theoretical value.
