Squares, Cubes & Roots

Grade 8 Math  ·  Powers and their inverses

🟦 Squaring and Cubing

When you square a number you multiply it by itself once. When you cube a number you multiply it by itself twice more (three factors total).

Square
n² = n × n
Cube
n³ = n×n×n
Square Root
√(n²) = n
Cube Root
∛(n³) = n

The square root (√) undoes squaring; the cube root (∛) undoes cubing.

📋 Perfect Squares & Cubes to Know

Perfect Squares (1 – 15)
1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49,
64, 81, 100, 121, 144,
169, 196, 225
Perfect Cubes (1 – 10)
1, 8, 27, 64, 125, 216,
343, 512, 729, 1000

Note: 64 appears in both lists — it is 8² and also 4³.

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • BRACKETS(−3)² = 9 (positive). But −3² = −9 because the square applies only to 3.
  • ESTIMATEBracket the unknown root between two consecutive perfect squares/cubes.
  • CUBE ROOTCube roots of negatives exist: ∛(−27) = −3 because (−3)³ = −27.
  • SYMBOL√ alone always means the positive (principal) square root.
