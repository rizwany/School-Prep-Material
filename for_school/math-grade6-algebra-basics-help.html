Algebra Basics

Grade 6 Math  ·  Variables, expressions, and simple equations

🔤 Key Algebra Concepts

Variable

A letter (x, y, n) that stands for an unknown number. e.g. x + 5 = 9

Expression

A combination of numbers and variables. No equals sign. e.g. 3x + 7

Equation

Has an equals sign — both sides are equal. e.g. 3x + 7 = 16

Like Terms

Terms with the same variable. 3x and 5x are like terms; 3x and 5y are not.

📐 Simplifying & Solving

Simplifying means combining like terms to write an expression more simply.

Combining Like Terms
3x + 5x = 8x  |  4y − y = 3y  |  3x + 4 + 2x − 1 = 5x + 3

Solving an equation means finding the value of the variable. Use inverse (opposite) operations to isolate the variable — whatever you do to one side, do to the other.

Inverse Operations
+ and − are inverses  |  × and ÷ are inverses

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Simplify: 3x + 5 + 2x − 1

1

Group like terms: (3x + 2x) + (5 − 1)

2

Combine: 5x + 4

3x + 5 + 2x − 1 = 5x + 4
2

Solve: x + 7 = 12

1

Subtract 7 from both sides to isolate x: x + 7 − 7 = 12 − 7

2

x = 5

x = 5   Check: 5 + 7 = 12 ✓
3

Solve: 3n = 18

1

Divide both sides by 3: 3n ÷ 3 = 18 ÷ 3

2

n = 6

n = 6   Check: 3 × 6 = 18 ✓
4

Solve: 2x − 5 = 9

1

Add 5 to both sides: 2x − 5 + 5 = 9 + 5 → 2x = 14

2

Divide both sides by 2: x = 14 ÷ 2 = 7

x = 7   Check: 2(7) − 5 = 9 ✓
5

A box has x books. After adding 4 more, there are 11 books. Find x.

1

Write the equation: x + 4 = 11

2

Subtract 4 from both sides: x = 11 − 4 = 7

x = 7 (there were 7 books to start)

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • LIKE TERMS ONLY You can only combine like terms. 3x + 4y cannot be simplified — different variables.
  • BALANCE Whatever you do to one side of an equation, you must do the same to the other side.
  • CHECK IT Always substitute your answer back into the original equation to verify it's correct.
  • COEFFICIENT In 5x, the number 5 is the coefficient. 1x is just written as x, not 1x.
