Geometry: Triangles & Congruence

Grade 7  ·  Triangle types, angle sum, congruence criteria

Practice Worksheet →
Types of Triangles
Equilateral
All sides equal

All 3 angles = 60°. Highest symmetry — 3 lines of symmetry.

Isosceles
2 sides equal

Base angles are equal. 1 line of symmetry.

Scalene
No sides equal

All angles different. No lines of symmetry.

Right-Angled
One 90° angle

Hypotenuse is the longest side. Can be isosceles or scalene.

Obtuse
One angle > 90°

The other two angles are always acute.

Acute
All angles < 90°

Equilateral triangles are always acute.

Key Angle Properties
Angle Sum
∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180°

The interior angles of ANY triangle always add up to 180°.

Exterior Angle
ext ∠ = sum of 2 remote interior

An exterior angle equals the sum of the two non-adjacent interior angles.

Isosceles Property
Base angles equal

If two sides are equal, the angles opposite them are equal.

Congruence Criteria (CPCT)

Two triangles are congruent if they are identical in shape AND size. CPCT = Corresponding Parts of Congruent Triangles.

SSS
Side-Side-Side

All three pairs of corresponding sides are equal.

SAS
Side-Angle-Side

Two sides and the included angle between them are equal.

ASA
Angle-Side-Angle

Two angles and the side between them are equal.

AAS
Angle-Angle-Side

Two angles and a non-included side are equal.

RHS
Right angle-Hypotenuse-Side

For right-angled triangles: hypotenuse and one other side equal.

Worked Examples
1. Find the missing angle: ∠A = 55°, ∠B = 72°, find ∠C
Angle sum = 180°
∠C = 180° − 55° − 72° = 180° − 127°
∠C = 53°
2. An isosceles triangle has a vertex angle of 40°. Find the base angles.
Base angles are equal. Let each base angle = b.
40° + b + b = 180°
2b = 140°, so b = 70°
Each base angle = 70°
3. Find the exterior angle if the two remote interior angles are 48° and 65°.
Exterior angle = sum of remote interior angles
= 48° + 65°
= 113°
4. Name the congruence criterion: triangles share two equal sides and the angle between them.
Two sides and the INCLUDED angle → this matches one criterion exactly.
SAS (Side-Angle-Side)
5. An equilateral triangle has a perimeter of 36 cm. Find one side.
All 3 sides equal. Side = perimeter ÷ 3
= 36 ÷ 3
= 12 cm
Quick check: After finding a missing angle, verify all three angles sum to exactly 180°.
