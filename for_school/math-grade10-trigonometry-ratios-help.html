Trigonometry: Ratios & Identities

Grade 10 Math  ·  6 ratios, Pythagorean identities & complementary angles

📐 The Six Trigonometric Ratios

In a right triangle with angle θ, opposite side O, adjacent side A, and hypotenuse H:

sin θ
O / H
cos θ
A / H
tan θ
O / A
cosec θ
H / O = 1/sin θ
sec θ
H / A = 1/cos θ
cot θ
A / O = 1/tan θ

Memory aid for the primary three: SOH-CAH-TOA

Tip for sin: values are √0/2, √1/2, √2/2, √3/2, √4/2 for 0°→90°. cos is the reverse order.

⛔ Trigonometric Identities

Pythagorean 1
sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
Pythagorean 2
1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
Pythagorean 3
1 + cot²θ = cosec²θ
Complementary
sin(90°−θ) = cos θ  |  tan(90°−θ) = cot θ
Complementary
cos(90°−θ) = sin θ  |  sec(90°−θ) = cosec θ

✏️ Worked Examples

1

In a right triangle, opposite = 5, hypotenuse = 13. Find all six ratios.

1

Adjacent = √(13²−5²) = √(169−25) = √144 = 12.

2

sin θ = 5/13, cos θ = 12/13, tan θ = 5/12.

3

cosec θ = 13/5, sec θ = 13/12, cot θ = 12/5.

sin=5/13, cos=12/13, tan=5/12, cosec=13/5, sec=13/12, cot=12/5
2

Prove: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 using a 3-4-5 triangle.

1

sin θ = 3/5, cos θ = 4/5.

2

(3/5)² + (4/5)² = 9/25 + 16/25 = 25/25 = 1 ✓

Identity verified
3

Evaluate: sin 30° × cos 60° + cos 30° × sin 60°.

1

sin 30° = 1/2, cos 60° = 1/2, cos 30° = √3/2, sin 60° = √3/2.

2

= (1/2)(1/2) + (√3/2)(√3/2) = 1/4 + 3/4 = 1.

= 1 (this is sin(30°+60°) = sin 90° = 1)
4

Simplify: sin(90°−θ) / cos(90°−θ).

1

sin(90°−θ) = cos θ and cos(90°−θ) = sin θ.

2

cos θ / sin θ = cot θ.

= cot θ
5

If tan θ = 3/4, find sin θ and cos θ.

1

tan θ = O/A = 3/4. Hypotenuse = √(9+16) = 5.

2

sin θ = 3/5, cos θ = 4/5.

sin θ = 3/5, cos θ = 4/5

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • RECIPROCALcosec θ = 1/sin θ, NOT 1/cos θ. The "co" in cosecant pairs with sine, not cosine.
  • UNDEFINEDtan 90° and cot 0° are undefined (division by zero). Don't use them in expressions.
  • IDENTITY USEAlways replace expressions using identities before evaluating. sin²θ = 1 − cos²θ is often the key step.
  • COMPLEMENTsin(90°−θ) = cos θ, not sin θ. The function name changes for complementary angles.
