Shapes & Patterns

In this guide, you'll learn to recognise 2D shapes by their sides and corners, and how to read, describe, and extend repeating patterns.

Section 1

2D Shapes

A 2D (flat) shape is a shape you can draw on paper. It has sides (straight edges) and corners (where two sides meet). A circle has no straight sides and no corners — it is perfectly round.

Circle
0 sides · 0 corners
Triangle
3 sides · 3 corners
Square
4 equal sides · 4 corners
Rectangle
4 sides · 4 corners
Pentagon
5 sides · 5 corners
Hexagon
6 sides · 6 corners

Count the sides (straight lines) and corners (points) to name any shape.

Section 2

Repeating Patterns

A pattern is a sequence that repeats using a core unit — the smallest part that keeps repeating. To extend a pattern, find the core and keep repeating it.

  1. 1
    Find the core: Look for the smallest repeating group. e.g., in 🔵🔴🔵🔴, the core is 🔵🔴 (AB pattern).
  2. 2
    Name the pattern: Use letters — A for the first shape/colour, B for the second, C for the third.
  3. 3
    Extend: Repeat the core to find what comes next. After A,B,A,B,A — the next is B.

Section 3

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Name by sides
A shape has 6 sides and 6 corners. What is it?
6 sides → hexagon
✅ Answer: Hexagon
Example 2 — Count corners
How many corners does a rectangle have?
Rectangle has 4 sides and 4 corners (right-angle corners)
✅ Answer: 4 corners
Example 3 — Extend a pattern
🔵 🟡 🔵 🟡 🔵 ___ — what comes next?
Core: 🔵 🟡 (AB pattern)
After 🔵, the next in the core is 🟡
✅ Answer: 🟡
Example 4 — Find the odd one out
Which shape has NO corners: triangle, circle, square?
Triangle → 3 corners. Square → 4 corners. Circle → 0 corners.
✅ Answer: Circle
Example 5 — Word problem
I am a flat shape. I have 3 corners and 3 sides. All my sides are the same length. What am I?
3 sides and 3 corners → triangle
All sides equal → equilateral triangle
✅ Answer: Triangle

Section 4

Watch Out! ⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Confusing square and rectangle: Thinking they are totally different
    ✅ A square IS a special rectangle where all 4 sides are equal. Both have 4 corners.
  • ❌ Saying a circle has 1 side: The curved edge counts as a side
    ✅ A circle has 0 straight sides and 0 corners. Its curved boundary is not a straight side.
  • ❌ Breaking a pattern by starting from 1 each time: In ●■▲●■▲●___, guessing ● instead of ▲
    ✅ Always find the core first (●■▲), then count where you are in it. The 7th item is ● (position 1 of core), so the 8th is ■ (position 2).
