Profit, Loss & Discount

Grade 7  ·  Cost price, selling price, profit %, loss %, discount

Practice Worksheet →
Key Formulae
Profit
Profit = SP − CP
Profit % = (Profit / CP) × 100
SP = Selling Price, CP = Cost Price
Loss
Loss = CP − SP
Loss % = (Loss / CP) × 100
Loss occurs when SP < CP
Discount
Discount = MP − SP
Discount % = (Discount / MP) × 100
MP = Marked Price (original price)
SP from CP & Profit %
SP = CP × (1 + Profit%/100)
SP = CP × (1 − Loss%/100)
Quick SP formula
CP from SP & Profit %
CP = SP / (1 + P%/100)
CP = SP / (1 − L%/100)
Finding the original cost
Sale Price after Discount
SP = MP × (1 − D%/100)
D% = Discount percentage
Worked Examples
1. A shopkeeper buys an article for $80 and sells it for $100. Find the profit and profit %.
Profit = SP − CP = 100 − 80 = $20
Profit % = (20 / 80) × 100 = 25%
Profit = $20, Profit % = 25%
2. A book is bought for $60 and sold for $45. Find the loss %.
Loss = CP − SP = 60 − 45 = $15
Loss % = (15 / 60) × 100 = 25%
Loss = $15, Loss % = 25%
3. Find the SP if CP = $200 and Profit % = 15%.
SP = CP × (1 + 15/100) = 200 × 1.15
SP = $230
4. A jacket is marked at $120. A 20% discount is offered. Find the sale price.
Discount = 20% of 120 = 0.20 × 120 = $24
SP = MP − Discount = 120 − 24 = $96
(Or: SP = 120 × 0.80 = $96)
Sale price = $96
5. An item is sold for $140 at a profit of 40%. What was the cost price?
SP = CP × 1.40
CP = SP / 1.40 = 140 / 1.40
CP = $100
Always calculate profit % and loss % on the Cost Price (CP), not the Selling Price. Discount % is always calculated on the Marked Price (MP).
