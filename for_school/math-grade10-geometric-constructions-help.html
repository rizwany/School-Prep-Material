Geometric Constructions

Grade 10 Math  ·  Compass & straightedge, similar triangles & tangents

📐 What Are Geometric Constructions?

Geometric constructions are drawings made using only a compass and a straightedge (unmarked ruler) — no measurements allowed except those set with the compass. They produce exact, mathematically perfect figures.

Key Grade 10 constructions involve dividing line segments in a given ratio, constructing similar triangles, and drawing tangents to circles.

✂️ Core Constructions

1. Divide a line segment AB in ratio m : n
Tools: compass, straightedge
  1. Draw ray AX at an acute angle to AB.
  2. Mark (m+n) equal arcs along AX: A₁, A₂, … A_{m+n}.
  3. Join A_{m+n} to B.
  4. Draw a line through A_m parallel to A_{m+n}B to meet AB at point P.
  5. P divides AB in ratio m : n.
2. Construct a triangle similar to given △ABC in ratio m : n
Case m < n (smaller) and m > n (larger)
  1. Draw ray BX at acute angle to BC.
  2. If ratio is m:n, mark max(m,n) equal arcs on BX.
  3. Join B_{max} to C. Draw parallel through B_m (or B_n) to get C′ on BC (or BC extended).
  4. Draw A′C′ ∥ AC to get the similar triangle A′B C′.
3. Tangent to a circle from an external point P
Given: circle with centre O, external point P
  1. Join OP. Find midpoint M of OP.
  2. Draw a circle with centre M and radius MO = MP.
  3. This new circle intersects the original at T₁ and T₂.
  4. PT₁ and PT₂ are the two required tangents.
4. Tangent at a point on a circle
Given: circle centre O, point T on circle
  1. Join OT (this is the radius).
  2. Extend OT beyond T.
  3. Draw a perpendicular to OT at T — this is the tangent at T.
💡 Key theorem used: The Basic Proportionality Theorem (BPT) — a line parallel to one side of a triangle divides the other two sides in the same ratio.

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Point P divides AB (10 cm) in ratio 3 : 2. How far is P from A?

1

Total parts = 3 + 2 = 5. Each part = 10/5 = 2 cm.

2

AP = 3 parts = 3 × 2 = 6 cm.

AP = 6 cm, PB = 4 cm
2

A triangle is to be constructed similar to △ABC with sides in ratio 3 : 4. How many arcs are marked on the ray?

1

Ratio is 3 : 4. The larger number determines the total arcs for the construction.

2

Mark max(3, 4) = 4 equal arcs on the ray.

4 arcs
3

In the tangent construction from external point P, OP = 10 cm and radius = 6 cm. Find the tangent length.

1

PT² = OP² − r² = 100 − 36 = 64.

2

PT = 8 cm.

Tangent length = 8 cm
4

In dividing AB in ratio 2 : 3, what is the total number of equal arcs needed?

1

Total arcs = m + n = 2 + 3 = 5.

5 arcs
5

AB = 12 cm. Q divides AB in ratio 1 : 3. Find AQ and QB.

1

Total parts = 4. Each part = 12/4 = 3 cm.

2

AQ = 1 × 3 = 3 cm. QB = 3 × 3 = 9 cm.

AQ = 3 cm, QB = 9 cm

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • RATIO ORDERIn ratio m : n, the point closer to A corresponds to m. AP/PB = m/n, so AP = m/(m+n) × AB.
  • EXTENDED LINEWhen the ratio has m > n (building a larger triangle), the base line must be extended beyond C before drawing the parallel.
  • MIDPOINT STEPIn the tangent-from-external-point construction, finding the midpoint of OP is the key step — don't skip it.
  • BPTThe entire similar-triangle construction relies on the Basic Proportionality Theorem. A line parallel to one side divides the other two sides proportionally.
