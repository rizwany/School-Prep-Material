Linear Equations (Two Variables)

Grade 8 Math  ·  Solving simultaneous equations

📐 What are Linear Equations in Two Variables?

A linear equation in two variables has the form ax + by = c, where a, b, c are constants and x, y are unknowns. One equation alone has infinitely many solutions (a whole line). A pair of simultaneous equations usually gives a unique solution — the point where two lines cross.

Standard Form
ax + by = c   (e.g. 2x + 3y = 12)

🔧 Solving by Substitution

Use substitution when one equation is easy to rearrange for a single variable.

Step 1 Rearrange one equation to isolate x (or y).
Step 2 Substitute that expression into the other equation.
Step 3 Solve the resulting single-variable equation.
Step 4 Substitute back to find the other variable. Check in both original equations.

➕ Solving by Elimination

Use elimination when both equations share a coefficient you can cancel.

Step 1 Multiply one or both equations so a variable has the same coefficient.
Step 2 Add or subtract the equations to eliminate that variable.
Step 3 Solve the remaining single-variable equation.
Step 4 Substitute back to find the other variable. Check both equations.

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Substitution: x + y = 10  and  x − y = 4

1

From Eq 1: x = 10 − y

2

Sub into Eq 2: (10 − y) − y = 4 → 10 − 2y = 4 → y = 3

3

x = 10 − 3 = 7

x = 7,   y = 3
2

Elimination: 2x + 3y = 16  and  2x + y = 10

1

Subtract Eq 2 from Eq 1: (2x+3y) − (2x+y) = 16 − 10 → 2y = 6 → y = 3

2

Sub y = 3 into Eq 2: 2x + 3 = 10 → 2x = 7 → x = 3.5

x = 3.5,   y = 3
3

Elimination with multiplication: 3x + 2y = 11  and  x + y = 4

1

Multiply Eq 2 by 2: 2x + 2y = 8

2

Subtract from Eq 1: (3x+2y) − (2x+2y) = 11 − 8 → x = 3

3

Sub x = 3 into Eq 2: 3 + y = 4 → y = 1

x = 3,   y = 1
4

Word problem: Two numbers add to 20; the larger is 4 more than the smaller.

1

Let x = larger, y = smaller. Equations: x + y = 20 and x = y + 4.

2

Sub: (y + 4) + y = 20 → 2y = 16 → y = 8

3

x = 8 + 4 = 12

The numbers are 12 and 8.
5

Check a solution: Does x = 2, y = 5 satisfy 3x + y = 11?

1

3(2) + 5 = 6 + 5 = 11 ✓

Yes — x = 2, y = 5 is a solution.

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • SIGNWhen subtracting equations, distribute the negative sign to every term in the second equation.
  • CHECKAlways substitute your answer back into BOTH original equations to verify.
  • LABELSDefine what x and y represent before setting up word-problem equations.
  • INFINITEIf both equations are the same line, there are infinitely many solutions (dependent). If they are parallel, there is no solution (inconsistent).
