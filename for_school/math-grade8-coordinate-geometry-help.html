Coordinate Geometry

Grade 8 Math  ·  Distance, midpoint, slope & lines

📐 Core Formulae

Given two points A(x₁, y₁) and B(x₂, y₂):

Distance Formula
d = √[(x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²]
Midpoint Formula
M = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2)
Gradient (Slope)
m = (y₂−y₁) / (x₂−x₁)
Equation of Line
y = mx + c

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Find the distance between A(1, 2) and B(5, 5)

1

d = √[(5−1)² + (5−2)²] = √[16 + 9] = √25

Distance = 5 units
2

Find the midpoint of A(2, 4) and B(8, 10)

1

M = ((2+8)/2, (4+10)/2) = (10/2, 14/2)

Midpoint = (5, 7)
3

Find the gradient of the line through (1, 3) and (4, 9)

1

m = (9 − 3) / (4 − 1) = 6 / 3

Gradient m = 2
4

Write the equation of a line with gradient 3 passing through (0, −1)

1

y = mx + c → m = 3, c = y-intercept = −1

y = 3x − 1
5

Find the gradient of y = −2x + 7. Are y = 2x + 1 and y = −½x + 3 perpendicular?

1

Gradient of y = −2x + 7 is −2.

2

Two lines are perpendicular if m₁ × m₂ = −1. 2 × (−½) = −1 ✓

Gradient = −2.   Yes, the lines are perpendicular.

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • ORDERFor distance and midpoint, keep (x₁,y₁) and (x₂,y₂) consistent — don't mix them up.
  • SQUARE ROOTAlways simplify inside the square root before taking it: √(9+16) = √25 = 5, not 3+4.
  • GRADIENTA horizontal line has gradient 0. A vertical line has undefined gradient.
  • PARALLELParallel lines have equal gradients but different y-intercepts.
