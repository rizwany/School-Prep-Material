Decimals (Operations)

Grade 5 Math  ·  Add, subtract, multiply & divide decimals

📌 Four Operations with Decimals

➕ Adding & ➖ Subtracting

Line up the decimal points. Add zeros as needed. Then add or subtract as whole numbers — keep the decimal point in the same column.

✖️ Multiplying

Ignore the decimal, multiply as whole numbers, then count total decimal places in both factors and place the point in the product.

➗ Dividing by a Whole Number

Keep the decimal point directly above in the quotient. Divide as normal whole-number long division.

➗ Dividing by a Decimal

Multiply both dividend and divisor by 10 (or 100) to make the divisor a whole number, then divide normally.

✏️ Worked Examples

1

3.46 + 1.7 (adding, different decimal places)

1

Write with decimal points aligned: 3.46 + 1.70 (add a trailing zero)

2

Add column by column right to left: 6+0=6, 4+7=11 (write 1, carry 1), 3+1+1=5

3.46 + 1.7 = 5.16
2

5.3 − 2.78 (subtracting)

1

Align decimals: 5.30 − 2.78 (add trailing zero to 5.3)

2

Subtract: 0 can't minus 8, borrow. 10−8=2; 2 can't minus 7, borrow. 12−7=5; 4−2=2

5.3 − 2.78 = 2.52
3

1.4 × 2.3 (multiplying decimals)

1

Multiply as whole numbers: 14 × 23 = 322

2

Count decimal places: 1.4 has 1, 2.3 has 1 → total 2 places

3

Place decimal: 322 → 3.22

1.4 × 2.3 = 3.22
4

6.45 ÷ 3 (dividing decimal by whole number)

1

Place decimal point directly above: set up 6.45 ÷ 3

2

6 ÷ 3 = 2; 4 ÷ 3 = 1 remainder 1; bring down 5 → 15 ÷ 3 = 5

6.45 ÷ 3 = 2.15
5

0.84 ÷ 0.4 (dividing by a decimal)

1

Multiply both by 10: 8.4 ÷ 4

2

8.4 ÷ 4 = 2.1

0.84 ÷ 0.4 = 2.1

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • ALIGN For addition and subtraction, always line up decimal points — not just digits. 3.5 + 1.25 must have the dots above each other.
  • TRAILING ZEROS Trailing zeros after the decimal don't change the value: 3.5 = 3.50. Add them to help align columns.
  • COUNT PLACES When multiplying, count decimal places in BOTH numbers and add them together.
  • MOVE POINT When dividing by a decimal, multiply both numbers by a power of 10 to eliminate the decimal in the divisor.
