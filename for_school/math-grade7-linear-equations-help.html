Linear Equations

Grade 7 Math  ·  Solving equations with one variable

⚖️ What is a Linear Equation?

A linear equation has one variable raised to the power of 1 (no x², x³, etc.). Both sides of the equation are equal — like a balanced scale.

Standard Form

ax + b = c, where a ≠ 0. The variable x appears once.

Golden Rule

Whatever you do to one side, do the same to the other. Keep it balanced!

Variables Both Sides

Collect all variable terms on one side, all numbers on the other.

Fractional Equations

Multiply both sides by the denominator to clear fractions first.

Strategy
Step 1: Clear brackets  |  Step 2: Collect like terms  |  Step 3: Isolate x

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Solve: 3x + 5 = 20

1

Subtract 5 from both sides: 3x = 20 − 5 = 15

2

Divide both sides by 3: x = 15 ÷ 3 = 5

x = 5   Check: 3(5) + 5 = 20 ✓
2

Solve: 2x − 7 = 11

1

Add 7 to both sides: 2x = 18

2

Divide by 2: x = 9

x = 9   Check: 2(9) − 7 = 11 ✓
3

Solve: 4x + 3 = 2x + 11 (variables on both sides)

1

Subtract 2x from both sides: 2x + 3 = 11

2

Subtract 3: 2x = 8

3

Divide by 2: x = 4

x = 4   Check: 4(4)+3 = 19 = 2(4)+11 ✓
4

Solve: (x + 3) / 2 = 5

1

Multiply both sides by 2: x + 3 = 10

2

Subtract 3: x = 7

x = 7   Check: (7+3)/2 = 5 ✓
5

A number is doubled and then 9 is added, giving 25. Find the number.

1

Write the equation: 2n + 9 = 25

2

2n = 16 → n = 8

The number is 8

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • BALANCEEvery operation must be applied to both sides of the equation — never just one side.
  • SIGNSWhen moving a term across the equals sign, its sign flips: +5 becomes −5.
  • CHECK ITAlways substitute your answer back to verify. This catches arithmetic slips.
  • FRACTIONSMultiply through by the denominator first to avoid working with fractions throughout.
