Rational Numbers

Grade 7 Math  ·  Operations with fractions and signed numbers

🔢 What are Rational Numbers?

A rational number is any number that can be written as a fraction p/q, where p and q are integers and q ≠ 0.

This includes: integers (−3, 0, 5), fractions (3/4, −2/5), terminating decimals (0.75), and repeating decimals (0.333…).

Addition / Subtraction

Find a common denominator, then add/subtract numerators.

Multiplication

Multiply numerators × numerators and denominators × denominators.

Division

Multiply by the reciprocal: a/b ÷ c/d = a/b × d/c

Comparing

Convert to a common denominator (or decimals) then compare.

Key Rules
(+) × (−) = −  |  (−) × (−) = +  |  Division: multiply by reciprocal

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • COMMON DENOMYou must have a common denominator to add or subtract fractions — never add top and bottom separately.
  • RECIPROCALTo divide by a fraction, flip it (find the reciprocal) and multiply. Don't just flip the first fraction.
  • SIGNSKeep careful track of negative signs. (−) × (−) = positive; (+) × (−) = negative.
  • SIMPLIFYAlways reduce your answer to its lowest terms by dividing top and bottom by the GCF.
