Money Basics — Coins & Notes

In this guide you'll learn the value of everyday coins and notes, how to add them up to find a total, and how to work out change when you buy something. These are real-life maths skills you can use every day!

Section 2

How to Count Money

  1. 1
    Sort by value. Start with the highest-value coins or notes and work down to the lowest.
  2. 2
    Add each coin's value. Count on from the running total as you pick up each coin: "25 … 35 … 40 … 41 cents".
  3. 3
    Finding change. Change = amount paid − price of item. Or count up from the price to the amount paid.
  • 4
    Check your answer. Change + price should equal the amount you paid.

    • Section 3

    Worked Examples

    Example 1 — Count a set of coins
    You have: 1 quarter, 1 dime, 1 nickel, 2 pennies. How much money is that?
    Quarter: 25¢
    + Dime: 25 + 10 = 35¢
    + Nickel: 35 + 5 = 40¢
    + 2 pennies: 40 + 1 + 1 = 42¢
    ✅ Total: 42¢
    Example 2 — Work out change
    A pencil costs 30¢. You pay with a 50¢ coin. How much change do you get?
    Change = amount paid − price
    Change = 50¢ − 30¢ = 20¢
    ✅ Change: 20¢
    Example 3 — How many coins?
    How many nickels make 25¢?
    Each nickel = 5¢
    5, 10, 15, 20, 25 → count 5 nickels
    ✅ 5 nickels = 25¢
    Example 4 — Can I afford it?
    You have 45¢. A rubber costs 60¢. Can you afford it?
    45¢ < 60¢ you don't have enough
    You need 60 − 45 = 15¢ more.
    ✅ No — you need 15¢ more
    Example 5 — Adding dimes and nickels
    You have 3 dimes and 2 nickels. How many cents is that?
    3 dimes = 3 × 10 = 30¢
    2 nickels = 2 × 5 = 10¢
    Total = 30 + 10 = 40¢
    ✅ Total: 40¢

    Section 4

    Watch Out! ⚠️

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    • ❌ Counting coins by number, not value: Thinking 5 pennies > 1 quarter
      ✅ Always count the value, not the number of coins. 1 quarter (25¢) > 5 pennies (5¢).
    • ❌ Confusing a dime and a nickel: A dime is smaller but worth more
      ✅ Dime = 10¢ (thin, small). Nickel = 5¢ (thick, larger). Size ≠ value!
    • ❌ Subtracting change the wrong way: Change = price − amount paid
      ✅ Change = amount PAID − PRICE. You get back the difference.
