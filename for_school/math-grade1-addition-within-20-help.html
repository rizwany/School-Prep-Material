Addition (within 20)

In this guide, you'll learn what addition means, how to count on from the bigger number, use the Make 10 strategy, and solve simple word problems with sums up to 20.

⏱ ~5 min read 📝 Jump to Worksheet →

Section 1

What is Addition?

Addition means putting groups together to find the total. When we add, the answer is always equal to or bigger than the numbers we started with.

The numbers being added are called addends. The result is called the sum. The symbol for addition is +.

addend + addend = sum
e.g.  4 + 3 = 7
+
=
7

4 blue dots + 3 green dots = 7 dots in total

Section 2

Addition Strategies

  1. 1
    Count All: Count every object from 1. Good for tiny numbers: ●●● + ●● → 1,2,3,4,5 = 5.
  2. 2
    Count On: Start with the bigger number and count on. 8 + 3 → start at 8, count "9, 10, 11". Answer: 11.
  3. 3
    Make 10: If one addend is close to 10, fill it up first. 9 + 5 → give 1 to 9 to make 10, then 10 + 4 = 14.
  4. 4
    Doubles: Memorise doubles: 1+1=2, 2+2=4, 3+3=6, 4+4=8, 5+5=10, 6+6=12, 7+7=14.

Section 3

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Count on
What is 7 + 4?
Start at 7 (bigger number)
Count on 4: 8, 9, 10, 11
✅ Answer: 11
Example 2 — Make 10
What is 8 + 6?
8 needs 2 more to make 10 split 6 into 2+4
8 + 2 = 10, then 10 + 4 = 14
✅ Answer: 14
Example 3 — Missing addend
5 + ___ = 9. What is the missing number?
Start at 5, count on to reach 9
6, 7, 8, 9 — that's 4 steps
✅ Answer: 4
Example 4 — Doubles
What is 6 + 6?
6 + 6 is a double memorise: double 6 = 12
✅ Answer: 12
Example 5 — Word problem
Tom has 9 crayons. His friend gives him 7 more. How many crayons does Tom have in all?
9 + 7 = ?
9 + 1 = 10, then 10 + 6 = 16 make 10 strategy
✅ Answer: 16 crayons

Section 4

Watch Out! ⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Counting the starting number again: For 6 + 3, saying "6, 7, 8, 9, 10" (that gives 10, not 9)
    ✅ Start AT 6, then count ON 3 more steps: 7, 8, 9. The answer is 9.
  • ❌ Forgetting that order doesn't matter: Thinking 3 + 8 is harder than 8 + 3
    ✅ 3 + 8 = 8 + 3 = 11. Always start with the bigger number to count on fewer steps.
  • ❌ Adding 0 incorrectly: Thinking 7 + 0 = 8
    ✅ Adding 0 to any number leaves it unchanged: 7 + 0 = 7.

Quick Reference

Addition symbol
+ means "plus" or "add" or "and"
Sum
The answer to an addition problem.
Count On
Start at the bigger number, count up by the smaller.
Make 10
Fill one number up to 10, then add the rest.
Doubles
1+1=2, 2+2=4, 3+3=6, 4+4=8, 5+5=10, 6+6=12, 7+7=14, 8+8=16, 9+9=18, 10+10=20
Any number + 0
Always equals the number itself: 9 + 0 = 9.
