Circles & Tangents

Grade 10 Math  ·  Tangent properties, chord theorems & angle relationships

🔵 Circle Terminology

Centre (O)
Fixed point equidistant from all points on the circle.
Radius (r)
Line segment from centre to any point on the circle.
Chord
Line segment joining two points on the circle. Diameter is the longest chord.
Tangent
Line that touches the circle at exactly ONE point (point of tangency).
Secant
Line that intersects the circle at TWO points.
Arc
Part of the circumference. Minor arc < semicircle; major arc > semicircle.

📐 Tangent Theorems

Theorem 1 — Radius ⊥ Tangent
The radius drawn to the point of tangency is perpendicular to the tangent. So ∠OPT = 90° where O is centre, P is point of tangency, T is on the tangent.
Theorem 2 — Equal Tangents from External Point
Two tangents drawn from an external point to a circle are equal in length. If PA and PB are tangents from P, then PA = PB.
Theorem 3 — Tangent-Radius Right Triangle
With external point P, centre O, tangent length PT, and radius r: OP² = PT² + r² (Pythagoras on triangle OPT).

📐 Chord & Angle Theorems

Perpendicular from Centre to Chord
The perpendicular from the centre bisects the chord. If OM ⊥ AB, then AM = MB.
Angle at Centre = 2 × Angle at Circumference
The angle subtended by an arc at the centre is double the angle subtended at any point on the remaining part of the circle.
Angles in Same Segment
Angles subtended by the same arc at the circumference are equal.
Angle in Semicircle
The angle subtended by a diameter at the circumference is always 90°.

✏️ Worked Examples

1

A tangent PA touches a circle (centre O, radius 5 cm) at A. OP = 13 cm. Find PA.

1

OA ⊥ PA (radius ⊥ tangent), so triangle OAP is right-angled at A.

2

PA² = OP² − OA² = 13² − 5² = 169 − 25 = 144.

3

PA = 12 cm.

PA = 12 cm
2

Two tangents from external point P touch the circle at A and B. PA = 3x+1 and PB = 5x−7. Find x.

1

Tangents from external point are equal: PA = PB.

2

3x+1 = 5x−7 → 8 = 2x → x = 4.

x = 4
3

A chord is 16 cm long. The perpendicular from the centre to the chord is 6 cm. Find the radius.

1

Perpendicular bisects chord → half-chord = 8 cm.

2

r² = 8² + 6² = 64 + 36 = 100 → r = 10 cm.

Radius = 10 cm
4

An arc subtends 80° at the centre. What angle does it subtend at the circumference?

1

Angle at circumference = ½ × angle at centre.

2

= ½ × 80° = 40°.

40°
5

In a circle, AB is a diameter and C is on the circumference. ∠CAB = 35°. Find ∠ABC.

1

∠ACB = 90° (angle in semicircle).

2

∠ABC = 180° − 90° − 35° = 55°.

∠ABC = 55°

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • 90°The radius to the point of tangency is ALWAYS perpendicular to the tangent — use this as your first step in every tangent problem.
  • EQUAL TANGENTSOnly tangents from the SAME external point are equal. Tangents from different points are not necessarily equal.
  • CENTRE vs CIRCUMFERENCEThe angle at the centre is DOUBLE the angle at the circumference, not half. Check which you are given.
  • DIAMETERThe angle in a semicircle is always 90° — this only applies when the chord is a diameter.
