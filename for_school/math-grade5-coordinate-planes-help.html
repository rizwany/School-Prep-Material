Coordinate Planes

Grade 5 Math  ·  Plot and read points on a grid

🗺️ The Coordinate Plane

A coordinate plane (also called a Cartesian plane) is a flat surface with two perpendicular number lines called axes.

The x-axis runs horizontally (left-right). The y-axis runs vertically (up-down). They cross at the origin (0, 0).

Every point is described by an ordered pair (x, y). The first number is always the x-value (move right), the second is the y-value (move up).

📌 How to Plot a Point

Ordered Pair
(x, y) → start at origin, go x units right, then y units up

Tip: Remember the order — along the corridor first, then up the stairs. x (horizontal) always comes before y (vertical).

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Plot the point (4, 2)

1

Start at the origin (0, 0)

2

Move 4 units to the right along the x-axis

3

Move 2 units up along the y-axis

Mark the point where you end up — that is (4, 2)
2

What are the coordinates of a point 5 right and 3 up from the origin?

1

x = 5 (right), y = 3 (up)

The point is (5, 3)
3

A point is at (0, 4). Where is it on the grid?

1

x = 0 — don't move right (stay on y-axis)

2

y = 4 — move 4 units up

The point sits directly on the y-axis, 4 units up
4

Which point is further from the origin: (3, 0) or (0, 3)?

1

(3, 0) is 3 units along the x-axis from the origin

2

(0, 3) is 3 units along the y-axis from the origin

They are the same distance from the origin (both 3 units away)
5

A square has corners at (1,1), (4,1), (4,4), and (1,4). What is its side length?

1

Count from (1,1) to (4,1) along the bottom: 4 − 1 = 3 units

Side length = 3 units

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • ORDER (x, y) — x is ALWAYS first. (3, 5) and (5, 3) are different points!
  • ORIGIN The origin is (0, 0) — not (1, 1). Count from 0, not from 1.
  • AXIS x-axis is horizontal, y-axis is vertical. Mix them up and you'll plot the wrong point.
  • CORRIDOR FIRST Move along the x-axis first, then up/down. "Along the corridor, then up the stairs."
