Perimeter

In this guide, you'll learn what perimeter means and how to calculate it for rectangles, squares, and other shapes by adding up all their side lengths.

⏱ ~5 min read 📝 Jump to Worksheet →

Section 1

What is Perimeter?

The perimeter of a shape is the total distance around the outside of that shape. Think of it like building a fence around a garden — the total length of fencing you need is the perimeter.

To find the perimeter, simply add up the lengths of all the sides. Perimeter is measured in units of length: centimetres (cm), metres (m), etc.

For regular shapes like squares and rectangles, we can use a quick formula instead of adding each side separately.

Rectangle: P = 22 cm
8+3+8+3 = 22
Square: P = 20 cm
5×4 = 20

Add up all side lengths to find perimeter. For a rectangle, opposite sides are equal.

Section 2

Step-by-Step Method

Rectangle: P = 2 × (L + W)
L = Length  |  W = Width
Square: P = 4 × S
S = Side length (all sides are equal)
  1. 1
    Identify the shape. Is it a square, rectangle, triangle, or other polygon? Count the sides.
  2. 2
    Note all side lengths. Find the length of every side. For a rectangle, opposite sides are equal (L, W, L, W).
  3. 3
    Add all sides together. Or use the formula for common shapes: rectangle = 2×(L+W), square = 4×S.
  4. 4
    Write the unit. Always include the unit of measurement in your answer (cm, m, km, etc.).

Section 3

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Square Perimeter
A square has a side length of 6 cm. What is its perimeter?
P = 4 × S
P = 4 × 6
P = 24 cm
✅ Answer: 24 cm
Example 2 — Rectangle Perimeter
A rectangle is 9 cm long and 4 cm wide. What is its perimeter?
P = 2 × (L + W)
P = 2 × (9 + 4)
P = 2 × 13 = 26 cm
✅ Answer: 26 cm
Example 3 — Irregular Polygon (Triangle)
A triangle has sides of 5 cm, 7 cm, and 9 cm. What is its perimeter?
P = 5 + 7 + 9 add all sides
P = 21 cm
✅ Answer: 21 cm
Example 4 — Missing Side Length
A rectangle has a perimeter of 30 cm and a length of 10 cm. What is its width?
P = 2 × (L + W)
30 = 2 × (10 + W)
15 = 10 + W
W = 15 − 10 = 5 cm
✅ Answer: Width = 5 cm
Example 5 — Word Problem
Ahmed wants to put a fence around a rectangular garden that is 12 m long and 7 m wide. How many metres of fencing does he need?
Fencing needed = perimeter
P = 2 × (12 + 7) = 2 × 19 = 38 m
✅ Answer: 38 metres

Section 4

Watch Out! ⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Forgetting opposite sides in a rectangle: Adding only 2 sides instead of all 4
    ✅ A rectangle has 4 sides. Opposite sides are equal, so always add L+W+L+W or use 2×(L+W)
  • ❌ Confusing perimeter with area: Multiplying the sides instead of adding them
    ✅ Perimeter = SUM of all sides (add). Area = product of length × width (multiply). Different concepts!
  • ❌ Forgetting the unit: Writing "P = 24" instead of "P = 24 cm"
    ✅ Always include the unit of length in your answer (cm, m, km, etc.)

Section 5

Quick Reference

Perimeter
Total distance around the outside of a shape.
Rectangle: P = 2(L+W)
L = length, W = width. Opposite sides are equal.
Square: P = 4 × S
All 4 sides of a square are equal in length.
Triangle
P = side1 + side2 + side3
Any Polygon
P = sum of all side lengths.
Missing Side
Total P − sum of known sides = missing side.
