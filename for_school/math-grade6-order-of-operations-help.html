Order of Operations

Grade 6 Math  ·  BODMAS — the correct order to calculate

🧮 What is BODMAS?

BODMAS tells you the order in which to perform operations in a calculation. Always follow this order — never just left to right!

B
Brackets
O
Orders (powers & roots)
D
Division
M
Multiplication
A
Addition
S
Subtraction
Key Priority Rules
D and M have equal priority → work left to right
A and S have equal priority → work left to right

✏️ Worked Examples

1

Calculate: 3 + 4 × 2

1

Multiplication first (M before A): 4 × 2 = 8

2

Then addition: 3 + 8 = 11

3 + 4 × 2 = 11  (NOT 14!)
2

Calculate: (3 + 4) × 2

1

Brackets first (B): 3 + 4 = 7

2

Then multiplication: 7 × 2 = 14

(3 + 4) × 2 = 14
3

Calculate: 20 ÷ 4 + 3²

1

Orders first (O): 3² = 9

2

Division (D): 20 ÷ 4 = 5

3

Addition (A): 5 + 9 = 14

20 ÷ 4 + 3² = 14
4

Calculate: 2 × (8 − 3) + 4² ÷ 8

1

Brackets: 8 − 3 = 5

2

Orders: 4² = 16

3

Multiply and Divide (left to right): 2 × 5 = 10,   16 ÷ 8 = 2

4

Addition: 10 + 2 = 12

2 × (8 − 3) + 4² ÷ 8 = 12
5

Calculate: 18 ÷ 3 × 2

1

Division and Multiplication have equal priority → work left to right.

2

18 ÷ 3 = 6, then 6 × 2 = 12

18 ÷ 3 × 2 = 12  (NOT 3!)

⚠️ Watch Out!

  • LEFT TO RIGHT When D and M (or A and S) appear together, always work left to right — not right to left!
  • BRACKETS FIRST Always solve everything inside brackets before anything else, no matter what.
  • POWERS "Orders" means powers and square roots. Evaluate 3² or √25 before multiply/divide.
  • COMMON TRAP 3 + 4 × 2 ≠ 14. Multiplication happens before addition! The answer is 11.
