Learn More About Verbal Reasoning

Verbal reasoning is the ability to understand written information, draw logical conclusions from text, and use vocabulary precisely — a core component of most academic and workplace aptitude tests, and a strong predictor of comprehension-heavy roles.

Verbal reasoning improves fastest through active reading (engaging critically with what you read) rather than passive reading, combined with deliberate vocabulary building.

To build this skill step-by-step:

  • Read Actively, Not Passively: While reading, pause periodically to summarize the argument in your own words; this is far more effective than simply re-reading.
  • Build Vocabulary in Context: Learn new words from the sentences they appear in, not isolated flashcard lists; context makes meaning stick better.
  • Practice True/False/Cannot-Say Questions: These test precise reading rather than general knowledge; practice distinguishing what a passage explicitly states from what merely seems plausible.
  • Time Yourself on Passages: Verbal reasoning tests are usually timed tightly; practicing under time pressure builds the speed that separates a good score from a great one.
