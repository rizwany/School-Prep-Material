Learn More About Depression Symptoms & Wellbeing

Depression is a common and treatable condition that affects mood, energy, sleep, and the ability to find pleasure in daily life. A single self-check-in can't diagnose depression, but understanding common symptoms helps you recognize when it's time to seek support.

Wellbeing isn't just the absence of depression — it's actively built through habits like connection, movement, purpose, and rest, all well-supported by research as protective factors.

Ways to understand and support yourself:

  • Learn the Core Symptom Categories: Understand the difference between situational low mood and persistent symptoms (two weeks or more) affecting sleep, appetite, energy, and interest.
  • Build a Daily Behavioral Activation Habit: Depression often reduces activity, which then worsens mood; scheduling one small, meaningful activity each day can interrupt that cycle.
  • Track Mood Patterns: A simple daily mood log helps you and a professional identify triggers, patterns, and what's actually helping.
  • Reach Out to a Professional: If symptoms persist most of the day, nearly every day, for two weeks or more, a doctor or mental-health professional can discuss evidence-based treatment options.
